# Thiruvasagam — Sensitive Content Log

Verses flagged during the Task A edit pass. These are noted for awareness — most are theologically intentional and should not be changed without careful consideration.

**Status:** Log complete for all 51 chapters. Task B reframes (Ch 38, 40, 51 women-as-obstacle verses) are still pending — see EDIT-NOTES.md for the full verse list.

## Ch 01 — Sivapuranam

**Verses 53–55**
Translation: "Wrapped in outer skin, covered everywhere with worm-like filth, this body — a miserable hovel with nine orifices that ooze impurity, while all five senses in turbulent confusion perform their endless deceptions"
Sensitive element: Graphic description of the body as decaying and filth-ridden; theologically intentional (bhakti body-abasement convention) but may disturb modern readers unfamiliar with the tradition.

**Verse 84**
Translation: "Dwelling within this strange, deformed, and repulsive body —"
Sensitive element: Same body-abasement convention as v53–55; the body described as alien and repulsive. Theologically intentional — do not soften.

## Ch 02 — Kirtti-t-Tiru-Agaval

**Verses 132–135**
Translation: "Some who could not come to attain him leaped into the fire! Some were overcome with divine madness and fell into ecstatic confusion! Some rolled on the earth, fell down, and cried out in divine ecstasy! Some rushed with swift legs, running and plunging headlong into the sea!"
Sensitive element: Devotees depicted self-harming (leaping into fire, plunging into the sea) in devotional frenzy. Theologically framed as ecstatic liberation — do not soften, but may require context for modern readers.

## Ch 03 — Tiru-Andap-Pakuti

**Verses 150–155**
Translation: "Crying out with unfailing love, bones melting — Swelling and roaring like the waves of the ocean, Heads reeling, falling and rolling, crying aloud — Becoming confused like madmen, intoxicated like the inebriated — The townspeople bewildered, those who heard filled with amazement — With the vast, unbridled frenzy of a rutting elephant —"
Sensitive element: Extended ecstatic-frenzy sequence (loss of bodily control, madness, intoxication) framed as devotional transport — same theologically intentional register as Ch 02 v132–135; do not soften.

## Ch 05 — Tiru-Ccatakam

**Verse 23**
Translation: "O barren heart that quivers and melts for dark-eyed, red-lipped, white-toothed women struck by spring's flower-arrows — the one who entered you and claimed you, melting all flesh, has now gone into heaven. Do you see? You are living on without dying!"
Sensitive element: Women framed as distractions from God (women-as-obstacle); poet addresses his own heart's susceptibility to physical beauty. Same pattern as Task B targets in Ch 38, 40, 51.

**Verse 31**
Translation: "I, solitary, tossed in the vast ocean of birth by massive waves, without any handhold — bewildered by the sway of those with lips like ripe fruit, fallen into the jaws of the great shark of desire"
Sensitive element: Women (evoked by "lips like ripe fruit") and desire personified as a predator. Women-as-obstacle framing.

**Verse 44**
Translation: "Not thinking at all of how spring's arrow pierces and the mind reels — churned like yogurt in the deceit of deer-eyed women — though my Lord performed honey-pervaded sacred grace: I do not go to enter his city of Shiva."
Sensitive element: Women explicitly blamed ("deceit of deer-eyed women") for the poet's failure to progress spiritually. Direct women-as-obstacle framing; more explicit than v23 and v31.

**Verse 61**
Translation: "here perceiving a woman's quivering lips, just her garment-edge loosening, her face pearled with tiny sweat: these perceiving, ruin has surrounded me!"
Sensitive element: Most explicit verse in Ch 05 — a close physical description of a woman (lips, loosening garment, dewy face) as the cause of spiritual ruin. Women-as-obstacle framing with graphic physical detail.

## Ch 06 — Neettal-Vinnappam

**Multiple verses throughout (approximately v1, v3, v5, v25, v36, v38, v40, v41, v46)**
Translation samples: "Women with full breasts pressed close, with kovvai-red lips — I cannot let go of them"; "I am like a tree on a riverbank, taking root deeper in the five senses through dark-eyed women"; "Like a moth drawn into blazing flame, I have fallen day after day among beguiling women"; "I am consumed going between the breast-hills of jewel-smiled women"; "I cannot escape the grip of fair-worded beautiful women"; "I fell into the net of women's glances and wandered stumbling"; "Plunging in the hot water of desire for women with crocodile-red mouths"; "Like a fly at the jackfruit-breasts of side-glancing women, I keep yearning."
Sensitive element: This is the most sustained women-as-obstacle chapter in the Thiruvasagam. The entire chapter (a release-plea to Shiva) is structured as an extended confession of entrapment by women, with repeated explicit physical catalogs (breasts, mouths, eyes, glances). Each verse ends "See that you release me." Theologically conventional self-mortification, but the density and physicality may read as significantly objectifying to modern readers. Consider whether to extend Task B reframes to cover this chapter.

## Ch 09 — Tiru-Porr-Cunnam

**Verses 8, 10, 13, 14 (approximate)**
Translation samples: "as your full breasts heave"; "As our pearl-adorned breasts sway and sway"; "O lovely women with golden jeweled breasts"; "as breasts heave with welling love"
Sensitive element: Unlike Ch 06, the speaker here is a group of women devotees singing a communal grinding-song — the physical descriptions apply to the devotee-women themselves, not to women as obstacles. The tone is joyful and collective. Less problematic than Ch 06, but the repeated breast imagery may still feel jarring to some modern readers.

## Ch 24 — Ataikkala-Pattu

**Verses 411–414**

Sensitive element: Four consecutive verses frame women as the agents of the poet's downfall (waves, traps, nets) — same women-as-obstacle pattern as Task B targets in Ch 38, 40, 51. Ch 24 is not currently on the Task B list; consider whether to extend reframes here.

## Ch 04 — Pottri-t-Tiru-Agaval

**Verse 3**
Translation: "Dark-tressed, red-lipped, white-toothed — women beautiful as dark peacocks, their combined grace pressing close, intoxicating — breasts rising free of the bodice, radiant, swelling forward, the waist aching under the weight, spreading on every side, so full that not a blade of grass could pass between them — from the piercing plunder of those sharp eyes — I survived."
Sensitive element: Explicit physical description of women's bodies framed as temptations the poet survived by grace — theologically conventional self-confession, but the catalog of female physical attributes may read as objectifying to modern readers.

## Ch 25 — Acai-Pattu

**Verses 419–422**
Translation: "Sinews swarming like ropes, brain, bones, and skin covering it — I cannot remain inside this body any longer"; "This small hut — this body — swarms with lice, buzzes with flies, and wanders about in filth"; "This walking cage — densely packed with bones, disgusting, oozing great filth, without vigor — clings to me and causes me suffering"; "This body of mine — wounded inside, covered outside with skin — I have been like a tamarind fruit: sour and rough."
Sensitive element: Extended body-abasement sequence across four consecutive verses — graphic descriptions of the body as swarming with insects, oozing filth, a cage, a sour fruit. Theologically intentional bhakti convention (disgust with the body as motivation for liberation); do not soften.

**Verse 427**
Translation: "Caught in the net of those with eyes as fierce as darting carp, I am worn down and exhausted — I the dog see no refuge anywhere."
Sensitive element: Women-as-obstacle framing — the poet describes himself as ensnared by women's fierce eyes, worn down and without refuge. Same pattern as Ch 05 v61, Ch 06 multiple verses, Ch 24 v411–414. Consider extending Task B reframes to include this verse.

## Ch 41 — Atpudha-Pathu

**Verses 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7, 10**
Translation samples: v1 — "pulled under in the whirlpool called 'women'"; v2 — "I wandered instead, head reeling, with sandalwood-fragrant women" (gloss adds "breasted"); v4 — "struck down by the arrows of dark-haired women's eyes, and lay there helpless"; v5 — "entangled with women, drunk on their allure"; v6 — "I lay entangled with women, drowning in the flood of lips and sweet talk"; v7 — "I lay struck helpless by the kohl-dark eyes of full-breasted women"; v10 — "preoccupied with the tricks and low conduct of thick-haired women, dreaming of their carp-fish eyes."
Sensitive element: This is the second densest women-as-obstacle chapter after Ch 06 — six of the ten verses name women (their eyes, lips, bodies, allure) as the cause of the poet's spiritual wandering and downfall, from which Shiva rescues him. Physical catalogs of female attributes appear repeatedly. Theologically conventional bhakti self-abasement; do not soften, but the density may distress modern readers.

## Ch 40 — Kulap-Pattu

**Verse 2**
Translation: "Though I commit countless foul sins through desire for the shoulders of pure-voiced women with waists narrow as drums — I will not perish, I will not be reborn."
Sensitive element: Women-as-obstacle framing — desire for women's bodies named as the source of sin. This is on the Task B reframe list.

**Verse 10**
Translation: "the Lord who shares body with the branch-like lady of round striped breasts" (gloss: "kommai-vari-mulai" = round striped breasts).
Sensitive element: Physical description of Parvati's breasts as part of Ardhanarisvara epithet. Same register as Ch 29 v462, Ch 39 v1 — theologically conventional.

Sensitive element: Women-as-obstacle framing — women's glances named as the cause of the poet's suffering and spiritual entrapment, from which Shiva's grace rescues him. This is on the Task B reframe list.

## Ch 35 — Acca-Pattu

**Verses 3, 4**
Translation: v3 — "I do not fear the sidelong glance of bangle-wearing women"; v4 — "I do not fear the speech of parrot-like women; I do not fear their crafty smiles."
Sensitive element: Women's glances and speech listed alongside serpents and spears as dangers the poet has transcended. Same women-as-temptation tradition as Ch 05/06, but inverted — the poet asserts he has risen above these distractions. Less problematic than the obstacle chapters; theologically framed as spiritual courage.

## Ch 29 — Arut-Pattu

**Verse 462**
Translation: "The hourglass-waisted, curling-haired woman — the marks of her twin breasts upon his chest, like two gleaming dots in the sky-fire of ash — two radiances resting there."
Sensitive element: Physical description of Parvati's breast-marks on Shiva's chest (Ardhanarisvara imagery — sacred union of male/female principles). Different register from women-as-obstacle; theologically intentional. Not problematic but may surprise readers unfamiliar with the tradition.

Sensitive element: Physical description of Parvati's breasts as part of a standard Ardhanarisvara epithet for Shiva. Same register as Ch 29 v462 — theologically conventional, not women-as-obstacle.

## Ch 26 — Aticiya-Pattu

**Verses 428, 432**
Translation: v428 — "I was wasting away in the company of firm-breasted women"; v432 — "Standing in the way of fragrant-haired women, I was ruining myself."
Sensitive element: Women-as-obstacle framing in two verses — women named as the direct cause of the poet's spiritual ruin and wasting. Same theologically conventional self-abasement as Ch 05 and Ch 06; do not soften.

**Verse 434**
Translation: "This false dwelling — rotten flesh-walls, worm-ridden, festering, oozing — I took as real and stood caught in the whirlpool-sea of suffering."
Sensitive element: Body-abasement imagery (rotten walls, worms, festering, pus) — same bhakti convention as Ch 01 and Ch 25; theologically intentional, do not soften.

## Ch 43 — Thiruvorthai

**Verse 8**
Translation: "who appears from fire for those who dwell by the great sea, and unites with women like living portraits"
Sensitive element: Gloss reads "ooviyana mangaiyar thool punarum" — women like painted figures, embracing their shoulders. The referent is ambiguous (could be mythological female devotees or a specific legend); different register from women-as-obstacle since these are recipients of Shiva's manifestation, not distractions from him. Not problematic but worth noting for context.

## Ch 49 — Thiruppadai Atchi

**Verse 3**
Translation: "Will not the troubles stirred by the red-lipped beauties of this world scatter away?"
Sensitive element: Women (gloss: "senduvar vaay madavaar" — red-lipped tender ones) listed as a source of "idar" (trouble/suffering) among a litany of sufferings that will cease if God comes. Mild women-as-obstacle framing embedded in a positive-conditional structure; less intense than Ch 05/06 because it is one item among many rather than the center of the verse.

## Ch 51 — Acho Pathu

**Verse 3**
Translation: "taking all falsehood as truth / in the pleasure of women with full breasts / I destined to be utterly enthralled"
Sensitive element: Women with full breasts named as the source of sensual enthrallment from which Shiva saves the poet. Women-as-obstacle framing; the Ardhanarisvara refrain in the same verse ("the Lord who holds woman as half his body") creates a theological tension — the same body that entangles is the body Shiva integrates. **On Task B reframe list.**

**Verse 5**
Translation: "I would have stood helpless, afflicted by the sidelong glance of women with cotton-soft feet"
Sensitive element: Women's sidelong glance named as the cause of affliction and near-ruin, from which grace saves the poet. Direct women-as-obstacle framing. **On Task B reframe list.**

**Verse 6**
Translation: "women with clustering hair and beautiful bangles / I who would fall upon their rounded breasts"
Sensitive element: Explicit physical description of women (hair, bangles, rounded breasts) as the object of the poet's destructive desire, from which Shiva severs him. Most explicit women-as-obstacle verse in this chapter. **On Task B reframe list.**

**Verse 7**
Translation: "in the delusion of women / I destined to sink and fall"
Sensitive element: Women equated with delusion (maya) as the force pulling the poet down. Less physically explicit than v6 but direct causal framing — women as delusion. **On Task B reframe list.**

**Verse 8**
Translation: "destined to fall in desire into the embrace of jeweled women"
Sensitive element: Physical description of women (jeweled) and the poet's desired union with them framed as the state Shiva rescues him from. Same register as v6. Ardhanarisvara refrain again in the same verse. **On Task B reframe list.**
