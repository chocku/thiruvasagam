Acai Pattu

ஆசைப்பத்து
Chapter 25 · Composed at Thirupperunthurai
← Ch. 24 All Chapters Ch. 26 →

Acai Pattu (The Ten of Longing) carries the subtitle "Nature of the Soul" (Atthuma Ilakkanam), suggesting that longing for God is what defines the soul's essential character. Each of the ten verses ends with the refrain "I long for this, do you see? O my Lord!" — direct, urgent, even impatient. Manikkavasagar describes his body with characteristic disgust — swarming with lice, wrapped in bones and skin, sour as tamarind — and pours out specific yearnings: to be called out of the body, to see Shiva's smiling face, to cast off the body and enter Sivapura, to hear Shiva's coral lips say "fear not." The chapter rises from disgust with the body to a pure cry for Shiva's grace, closing with the devotee caught in the net of desire, seeing no refuge anywhere, and longing for the one word that will release him.

O Supreme One, O Supreme Light whom the people of the world and the celestials praise and hymn — Come! O One who gives the world of no return and comes to take me as your own — I long to roam, praising all your thousand names, calling out "our Great Lord!" in hymn. O Nectar that never satisfies the desire for more! Do you see, O my Lord!
