கோயில் மூத்த திருப்பதிகம்
Chapter 21 · Composed at Chidambaram (Ponnambalam — the golden hall)
← Ch. 20 All Chapters Ch. 22 →

This is an anguished personal hymn addressed directly to Shiva as the cosmic dancer at the golden hall of Chidambaram. Unlike the playful folk-songs that preceded it, this chapter is raw and urgent — a soul crying out to a God who seems silent. Manikkavasagar opens with a brilliant concentric logic: Parvati dwells in Shiva, Shiva dwells in Parvati, and both dwell in me — therefore, grant me the grace to dwell among your devotees. From there the poet's voice grows more desperate: he has been waiting like a heron watching for prey, night and day. He sees the other devotees — laughing, exulting, gathering in joy, spreading the sacred words — and he stands apart, a dog, a fox among lions. If Shiva continues to ignore him and he dies, won't people laugh? The chapter builds to a sustained cry: "Have compassion, O One who owns me!" Each verse closes with a reference to the golden hall — Ponnambalam, Chidambaram, the Stage where Shiva dances.

1

Utaiyaaḷ-un-than-natuvirukkum · Utaiyaaḷ-natuv-uḷ-nii-iruththi · Atiyeen-natuv-uḷ-iruuvirum-iruppathaanaal · atiyeen-un · Atiyaar-natuv-uḷ-irukkum · aruḷai-puriyaay · Ponnambalaththu-em · Mutiyaa-muthalee · en-karuththu-mutiyum-vannam-mun-nindree

Parvati dwells in the midst of You, and You are placed in the midst of Parvati. Since both of you dwell in the midst of me, Your servant — bestow on me the grace to dwell in the midst of Your devotees! O our undying Source of the golden hall of Chidambaram — stand before me and fulfill my heart's intent.
2

You once stood before me and claimed me. I too strove for that very thing. Now I stand behind, doing service. But I have fallen behind, O great Lord! If You don't graciously call to me saying "Come, stand, depart" — won't Your devotees, thinking of You, say "Who is this one?" O You who love the dance of the golden hall!
3

Uganthaanee = O-one-who-was-pleased · anbuutai-atimaikku = with-loving-service · urugaa = not-melting · uḷḷaththu = in-the-heart · unarvileeyeen = I-who-lack-awareness · Sagam = world · thaan = itself · ariya = to-know · muraiyittaal = if-I-cry-out-publicly · thakka-vaaru = proper-manner · andru = not · ennaaroe = won't-they-say · Magam = sacrifice · thaan = itself · sheithu = having-done · vazhivanthaer = those-who-came-in-succession · vaazha = to-thrive · vaazhthaay = You-lived-on · atiyeerkku = for-me-Your-servant · Mugam = face · thaan = indeed · thaaraa-vitil = if-You-don't-give · mutiveen = I-will-perish · Ponnambalaththu = of-the-golden-hall · em = our · muzhimuthalee = O-Complete-Source

O One who was pleased with loving service! I am one who lacks the melting awareness in my heart. If I cry out publicly for the whole world to see, won't they say it is improper? Those who came in succession doing sacrifice have thrived and lived on — but if You don't give me Your face, O our Complete Source of the golden hall, I will perish.
4

Muzhimuthalee-aimbulanukku-m · muuvarkkum-en-thanakkum · Vazhimuthalee · nin-pazha-vatiyaar-thiraḷ · vaan-kuzhumikk · Kezhimuthalee · aruḷ-thandhi-rukka · irangung-kolloe-endru · Azhumathuvee-andri · matru-en-sheygeen · Ponnambalaththu-araishee

Muzhimuthalee = O-Complete-Source · aimbulanikku = for-five-senses · muuvarkkum = for-three(Brahma-Vishnu-Rudra) · en-thanakkum = and-to-me · Vazhimuthalee = O-Source-of-the-path · nin = Your · pazha = ancient · vatiyaar = devotees · thiraḷ = gathering · vaan = sky/heaven · kuzhumiikk = crowding-together · Kezhimuthalee = O-Source-that-rises-together · aruḷ = grace · thanthi-rukka = having-given-and-staying · irangung-kolloe = will-it-have-compassion · endru = saying · Azhumathuvee = weeping-only/that-itself · andri = other-than · matru = moreover · en-sheygeen = what-shall-I-do · Ponnambalaththu = of-the-golden-hall · araishee = O-King

O Complete Source of the five senses, the three — Brahma, Vishnu, and Rudra — and of me! O Source of the path! Your ancient devotees gather and crowd together like the sky. Thinking "Will it have compassion and give grace and remain?" — other than weeping, what else can I do? O King of the golden hall!
5

Araishee-Ponnambalaththaatum-amuthee · endru-un-aruḷ-nookki · Irai-theer-kokkoththu · iravupagal-eesatru-iruntee · veesaktrreen · Karai-seer-atiyaar-kaḷi-shirappack-kaattshi-kotuththu · un-atiyeen-paal · Pirai-seer-paalin-ney-poolap-peesaa-thirunthaal · eesaaroe

Araishee = O-King · Ponnambalaththaatum = who-dances-in-the-golden-hall · amuthee = O-nectar · endru = saying · un = Your · aruḷ = grace · nookki = watching/awaiting · Irai-theer = seeking-prey · kokku = heron · oththu = like · iravupagal = night-and-day · eesatrru = having-grown-weary · iruntee = having-been · veesaktrreen = I-have-become-spent · Karai-seer = reaching-the-shore · atiyaar = devotees · kaḷi = joy · shirappa = excelling · kaattshi = vision · kotuththu = giving · un-atiyeen-paal = towards-me-Your-servant · Pirai-seer-paalin = of-milk-mixed-with-curds · ney = ghee · poola = like · peesaa-thirunthaal = if-You-stay-without-speaking · eesaaroe = won't-they-blame

O King who dances in the golden hall, O nectar — awaiting Your grace, I have become spent and exhausted, day and night, like a heron standing still in search of prey. You give vision to Your devotees who reach the shore and rejoice with abundant joy. But if You stay without speaking to me, Your servant — like ghee that separates from curdled milk — won't they blame You?
6

ஏசா நிற்பர் என்னை உனக்கு அடியான் என்று · பிறரெல்லாம் · பேசா நிற்பர் · யான்தானும் பேணா நிற்பேன் · நின்னருளே · தேசா நேசர் சூழ்ந்திருக்கும் · திருவோலக்கம் சேவிக்க · ஈசா பொன்னம்பலத்தாடும் எந்தாய் · இனித்தான் இரங்காயே

All others will stand without blaming me, saying "He is Your servant." They will stand without speaking. And I too will stand, caring for nothing but Your grace alone. To attend Your sacred court surrounded by devoted friends who have come from distant lands — O Lord who dances in the golden hall, O my Father, now indeed have compassion on me!
7

இரங்கும் நமக்கு அம்பலக்கூத்தன் என்றென்றே · மாந்திருப்பேனை · அருங்கற்பனை கற்பித்தாண்டாய் · ஆள்வாரிலி · மாடாவேனோ · நெருங்கும் அடியார்களும் நீயும் · நின்று நிலாவி விளையாடும் · மருங்கே சார்ந்து வர · எங்கள் வாழ்வே வாவென்று அருளாயே

Irangum = will-have-compassion · namakku = for-us · ambalakuuththan = dancer-of-the-hall · endrendree = saying-again-and-again · maanthi = deluded/content · iruppeenai = me-who-was-waiting · Arungkarpanai = rare-and-wondrous-imagination · karpithhaantaay = having-taught · aaḷvaarili = without-master · maataaveenooe = will-I-not-be-capable · Nerunggum = crowding/pressing-close · atiyaarkkaḷum = devotees-also · niiyum = and-You · nindru = standing · nilaavi = dwelling · viḷaiyaadum = playing/sporting · Marunggee = beside/near-that · saarnthu = coming-near · vara = to-come · engaḷ-vaazhvee = O-our-Life · vaavandru = saying-"come" · aruḷaayee = please-bestow-grace

You taught me rare and wondrous imagination and claimed me — me who had been deluded, waiting again and again thinking "The dancer of the hall will have compassion on us!" Will I not be capable even without a master? Where You and the crowding devotees stand together, dwell, and play — to come near that very place: O our Life, graciously say "Come!"
8

Aruḷaathozhinthaal = if-You-continue-not-granting-grace · atiyeenai = me-Your-servant · anjal = fear-not · enpaar = those-who-will-say · aar = who · inguup = here · Poruḷaa = as-of-worth/value · ennai = me · pugunthu = entering · aanta = claimed · Ponnee = O-Gold · Ponnambalakuuththaa = O-dancer-of-the-golden-hall · Maruḷaar = full-of-delusion · manaththootu = with-mind · unai = You · pirinthu = separated-from · varunthuveeenai = me-who-agonizes · vaavandru = saying-"come" · Theruḷaar = of-clarity · kuuttam = gathering/company · kaattaayeel = if-You-don't-show · sethtee-poonaarrr = if-I-have-gone-and-died · shiriyaaroe = won't-they-laugh

If You continue without granting grace, who here will tell me "Fear not"? O Gold who entered and claimed me as one of worth! O Dancer of the golden hall — if You don't show me Your gathering of clear-minded devotees, saying "Come!" to me who agonizes with a deluded mind, separated from You — if I go and die, won't they laugh?

Those who laugh with joy, those who exult, those who taste the sweetness — gathering and gathering, spreading Your sacred words, listening, praising; sitting in different places, bearing Your sacred name in their hearts, crying "O Lord who dances in the golden hall!" — before all of them, should I, the dog-like fox, just sit? O Trustworthy One, now indeed bestow Your grace!

Nalkaaathozhiyaan = He-who-will-not-fail-to-grant · namakku = for-us · endrum = always · naamam = name · pithatri = babbling/muttering · nayanam = eyes · niir = water/tears · malkaa = filling/overflowing · vaazhththaa = without-praising · vaay = mouth · kuzharaa = faltering · vananggaa = without-worshipping(with-hands) · manaththaal = with-the-mind · ninainthu = having-thought · urugi = melting · Pal-kaal = many-times · unnai = You · paavitthu = having-contemplated · paravi = having-praised · Ponnambalam = golden-hall · endree = saying-only · Olkaa = unshakeable/unwavering · nirkkum = standing · uyirkkku = for-the-soul/life · iranggi = having-compassion · aruḷaay = bestow-grace · ennai = me · utaiyaanee = O-One-who-owns-me

"He will not fail to grant us grace, ever" — muttering His name, eyes filling with tears, mouth faltering without praising aloud, not worshipping with hands but melting inwardly with the mind — many times contemplating You, many times praising, crying "Golden hall!" alone — for this unshakeable, unwavering soul, have compassion and bestow Your grace, O One who owns me!
