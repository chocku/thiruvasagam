Tirupandi Patikam

திருப்பாண்டிப் பதிகம்
Chapter 36 · Composed at Thirupperunthurai
← Ch. 35 All Chapters Ch. 37 →

Tirupandi Patikam (The Sacred Hymn of the Pandya Lord) is a ten-verse proclamation of Shiva's incarnation as the divine Pandya king who rides a horse. The chapter draws on the legend central to Manikkavasagar's conversion: Shiva appeared as a Pandya king at Thirupperunthurai, rode out on a wondrous horse, drew the bright sword of wisdom from its sheath, and devoured the poet's very soul. The chapter's imagery is almost martial — a king on horseback, enemies of rebirth rolling on the broad earth, the sword of wisdom cutting through karma — yet the purpose is pure liberation. The Pandya king on horseback presses his devotees into an undying flood of bliss, shows inexhaustible compassion, removes fierce karma, cuts the clinging of ancient impurity, and hands over the entire world. The chapter is also an urgent summons addressed to an audience that has not yet grasped what is on offer: "come while there is time," "hasten," "go foremost," "firmly join his sacred red feet." The final verse turns the legend into a closing image: Shiva and the great goddess Uma came to a certain Pandya king, mounted, and consumed his precious soul — O powerful, singular warrior, you without clarity, firmly join those sacred red feet.

My heart knows no form other than him alone — the one who shares his form with Uma, daughter of the mountain, the perfect nectar of the Pandya king, the incomparable, the one who has nothing. He stands revealed, melts those who bow at his anklet-adorned feet, and mounts the horse as a warrior.

Those who draw near him forget their cleverness and are seized by the madness of divine knowing — we have proclaimed this! The light that eclipses the sun grasps the reins and comes riding. When he comes on horseback and joins you — see the ruin of the household of rebirth! The King of Madurai will intercept and drive away all further birth.

You who have hearts that swim and bathe in the flood of bliss — so that the whole world might receive that flood, the Pandya king who mounted the horse took the form of a single wave of bliss and captured the hearts of his devotees. Go into that great flood of bliss and cherish his anklet-adorned feet!

Good people, do not go near the dense cycle of rebirth! The Lord of the good Pandya land is rising — and now, above all other times, is his time. He draws the bright sword of wisdom from its sheath, rides forward with supreme bliss, strikes down rebirth, and those who stand against him roll on the broad earth.

While there is time, love him and be saved! He is impossible even for Vishnu who swallowed the world, even for Brahma, even for the celestial gods to approach — he who swallowed the poison, our Lord of Pandya. He distributes the primal treasury to his devotees. Come and hasten!

He is the kindled light that makes the gathered darkness of illusion perish and all things shine clear — a light that even the Pandya king himself cannot fully describe. When you desire it, there is no barrier; access is immediate. Desire his feet! This is the gift of liberation the Pandya king is granting.

He mounts the wondrous horse and takes you in his hands — and the enemies called "this birth" go and are destroyed. For those who have entered his presence, he grants his own chosen, rare, and immeasurably great grace. Join the sacred red feet of the Pandya, the Southern king of boundless giving!

He presses you into a flood of bliss that will never perish, shows his inexhaustible compassion, removes the fierce karma, cuts the clinging of ancient impurity, and rules you as his own — that is the great sacred feet of the Pandya. His gift is to give you the entire world. Go and hasten!

So that his wide-spreading devotees might cross the great ocean of mixed evil karma and the births that follow, the Supreme Pandya king who melts bones comes on horseback. When he comes, those tender vine-like ones whose minds were captured stand rooted like trees and forget themselves entirely.
