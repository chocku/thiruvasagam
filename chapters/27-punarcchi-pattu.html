Punarcchi Pattu

புணர்ச்சிப் பத்து
Chapter 27 · Composed at Thirupperunthurai
Longing
← Ch. 26 All Chapters Ch. 28 →

Punarcchi Pattu (The Ten of Union) carries the subtitle "Nature of Non-Duality" (Attuvita Ilakkanam). Each verse describes a state of devotion the speaker longs to enter — standing by Shiva's side, melting in prayer before devotees, lying prostrate with flowers, embracing the feet, trembling with joy, entering and standing within. Every verse closes with the same haunting refrain: "when will I be doing this, united with my precious gem?" The precious gem is Shiva himself. Non-duality is the destination declared in the subtitle, but this chapter lives entirely in the longing that precedes it — asking the same question ten times, with mounting intensity, never answering it.

He who is a radiant golden mountain, a pearl to be worn — whom I have served so worthlessly —
who took in the lowest of the low — me, and is the refuge of grace:
whom even dark Vishnu and Brahma still cannot approach; who gave himself — my precious nectar —
when will I be standing close by him, united with my precious gem?

I cannot endure it, O King — I the servant, with senses fixed on things of this earth.
Making a mind that does not sink in the mud, praising "Shiva, our Great Lord!" —
melting within, layer after layer, like sand from which a spring has broken forth,
crying out "Hail!" and standing in worship — when will I be doing this, united with my precious gem?

That flame which towered up making tall Vishnu and Brahma tremble —
who drew me in — the unwilling one: my precious nectar —
before devotees with hearts overflowing, crying out aloud as long as I wish,
strewing fragrant flowers and lying prostrate — when will I be doing this, united with my precious gem?

Him whose name Brahma of the lotus, Vishnu, the others, and Indra king of the gods all speak and praise;
the flame that transcends word and meaning;
like a gooseberry, like honey, like milk — the nectar of fullness, the very taste of nectar:
to embrace and be united — when will I be doing this, united with my precious gem?

To behold those feet and crown shining ever more brightly — Brahma dug below and Vishnu flew above
yet neither could see that Lord — who, over all this great earth,
manifestly took my service and claimed me, saying "Ah! Ah!" in wonder:
to praise all his manner — when will I be doing this, united with my precious gem?

That fire — difficult for others to contemplate — water, wind, earth, sky:
the alone one who has no equal:
gazing with overflowing heart, throat choked with tears cascading in a flood,
hands clasped, adorning him with fragrant flowers — when will I be doing this, united with my precious gem?

laughing, weeping, worshipping, singing praises, dancing and proclaiming in every way,

O Father! O Mother to all twenty-one worlds! O Wise One who enrolled me, the dog —
O medicine for the disease of rebirth, great honey forever overflowing!
Praising again and again "O gem of all qualities!" — day and night
