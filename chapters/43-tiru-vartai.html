Thiruvorthai

திருவார்த்தை
Chapter 43 · Composed at Thiruperundurai
Praise
← Ch. 42 All Chapters Ch. 44 →

Thiruvorthai (The Sacred Word) is a ten-verse meditation on Shiva's manifold manifestations across time and legend. Each verse accumulates a cluster of divine acts — the hunter waiting at Mahendragiri, the archer who burned the triple cities, the one who crossed the ocean to Lanka, who seizes the great Sangam at Madurai — and closes with the same refrain: "those who know this [nature / manner / state / form] shall be our Lord!" The word "vorthai" (வார்த்தை) means word, speech, or saying — the chapter itself is a sacred testimony to what Shiva does and who he is. The theology is documentary rather than devotional: Shiva is recognized through his acts, and knowing those acts is itself a form of belonging to him.

he whose side the beautiful goddess came to share (Ardhanari) · versed in the Vedas / the sacred speaker · the light that dwells in the great lotus · faultlessly — supreme compassion — devotees — the joy-bringing justice / the principle that makes devotees rejoice — bestowing as very nature · garden of blossoming flowers — our holy Lord of Thiruperundurai · descending to earth · to reveal the primordial Absolute (Brahman) · those who know through grace — they shall be our Lord!

He who shares his side with the beautiful goddess, who is versed in the Vedas and speaks all sacred words,
the light that dwells in the great lotus — faultlessly bestowing supreme compassion as the very nature that makes devotees rejoice —
our holy Lord of the flower-blossoming Thiruperundurai, who descended to earth
to reveal the primordial Absolute: those who know this through grace shall be our Lord!

Vishnu and Brahma and Indra the king of the gods too · coming and worshipping · upon them — bestowing grace — the Lord (Isa) · in this world — descending / coming down · showing the good path · radiant with goodness · beautiful jeweled mansions — tall and flourishing — in that place (Thiruperundurai) · for the gentle noble-hearted woman · with exceeding virtue · the manner of bestowing compassion · those who know — they shall be our Lord!

When Vishnu, Brahma, and Indra came and bowed, he the Isa bestowed grace upon them all;
descending into this world, he showed the righteous path — and in that flourishing town of jeweled mansions,
he bestows supreme compassion with great virtue upon the gentle, noble-hearted:
those who know the manner of this bestowing shall be our Lord!

adorned crown — primal — king of the immortals · dancer of cosmic bliss (Nataraja) · the six religions — making bow / submit · riding the serpent's hood · the earth and the heavens too — spreading everywhere — praising · granting the end of all affliction — our greatly gracious Lord of Thiruperundurai · delighting in the feminine half (Ardhanari) · taking the jeweled net · scattering the stars like fish across the sky · those who know this manner — they shall be our Lord!

The primordial crowned king of the immortals — the dancer of cosmic bliss — who makes the six religions bow,
riding the serpent's hood as earth and heaven together spread his praise,
granting the end of all affliction — our greatly gracious Lord of Thiruperundurai,
who delights in the feminine half and takes the jeweled net to scatter the stars like fish across the sky: those who know this manner shall be our Lord!

Taking the form of a hunter at Mahendragiri, he sat while the gods came with their great petition to seek him —
that great Lord Shiva, so that we his servants might be saved through contemplation,
mounting his great horse — he who loves the dance — at ancient Thiruperundurai in those days,
enrolling everyone everywhere as his servants: those who know this nature shall be our Lord!

The archer who burned and destroyed the triple cities, appearing as a hunter ringed by fierce dogs,
with the gods performing acts of service before him — our great Lord in the roaming forest
showed compassion for the arrow-struck boar; our father at ancient Thiruperundurai in those days
