Atpudha Pathu

அற்புதப் பத்து
Chapter 41 · Composed at Thiruperundurai
← Ch. 40 All Chapters Ch. 42 →

Atpudha Pathu — "The Decade of Wonder" — is ten acts of astonished gratitude. In each verse Manikkavasagar describes his own utter degradation — caught in the ocean of life, wandering with women, drowning in karma, lying helpless like a mangy dog — and then, in a sudden turn, the incomprehensible moment Shiva intervened, showing his sacred feet and claiming him. The refrain alternates between two forms: "that wonder, how can I speak it?" (verses 1–2) and "I cannot fathom this wonder" (verses 3–10), suggesting the event exceeds both language and understanding. Composed at Thiruperundurai, the place of Manikkavasagar's own first encounter with Shiva, each verse returns to that original moment from a different angle of unworthiness. The mood is not shame but stunned, joyful incredulity: that someone as lost as this was seized, made into light, and claimed — that is the wonder.

caught in bewilderment — in this earthly world · trapped in the ocean called "life" · drawn into the whirlpool called "women" · I — my head reeling · to shed all falsehood — bestowing holy grace · showing his very golden feet · standing before me as Truth itself — revealing the open expanse · that wonder — how can I speak it!

Caught in bewilderment, trapped in this earthly world —
lost in the ocean called "life," pulled under in the whirlpool called "women" —
my head reeling with no direction,
he bestowed his holy grace to free me from all that falsehood,
showed me his golden feet,
and stood before me as Truth itself, revealing the open expanse of Reality.
That wonder — how can I speak it?

placing fitting great flowers — without completing · without worshipping with the right nature · with sandalwood-fragrant-breasted fine women · becoming head-spinning and wandering · so that I enter no more sorrow — bestowing grace · showing the golden anklet-feet · as the King — in open space — who stood before me · that wonder — how can I speak it!

I placed flowers in worship — but never with the right heart,
never completing the act, never bowing with true devotion.
I wandered instead, head reeling, with sandalwood-fragrant women.
So that I might enter no more sorrow,
he bestowed his grace — showed me his golden anklet-feet —
and stood before me in open space, as the King.
That wonder — how can I speak it?

performing falsehood in this world — doing many deceptions · standing in the gripped doorway of "I / mine" illusion · ranting with accumulated past karma — me who wandered · who seized me and stood before · the rare truth the great Vedas have sought · this servant — tapping repeatedly · who fed jaggery — sweetness — before me · I cannot fathom this wonder!

Acting and deceiving, piling up falsehood in this world,
I stood trapped in the jaws of "I" and "mine" —
ranting with old karma, wandering on and on.
He seized me.
That rarest of truths — the one the great Vedas have spent themselves searching for —
seized me, tapped me, again and again,
and fed me sweetness like jaggery.
I cannot fathom this wonder.

these births and deaths that naturally come — without thinking of them · speaking only falsehood — going on · those with dark hair — struck by their eyes · becoming agitated — me who lay helpless · the perfect sacred red feet — the anklets ringing · not withdrawing from grace · as my rare precious companion — claiming me as servant — who bestowed grace · I cannot fathom this wonder!

These births and deaths — I never thought of them at all.
I wandered on, speaking only falsehood,
struck down by the arrows of dark-haired women's eyes,
and lay there helpless, churned to confusion.
His perfect sacred red feet came, anklets ringing,
and he did not withdraw — did not leave —
but became my rare and precious companion,
claimed me as his own, bestowed his grace.
I cannot fathom this wonder.
5

cattle and kin and other pleasures · joining with women · struck by those alluring qualities — roaming in attachment — me who wandered · giving liberation — making my fierce deeds fall away · showing the soft-flower anklet-feet · making me dance — entering my very heart · who claimed · I cannot fathom this wonder!

Cattle, kin, and every pleasure in between —
entangled with women, drunk on their allure —
I roamed, enslaved to all of it, wandering on.
He gave me liberation. He made my fierce deeds fall away.
He showed me his soft-flower anklet-feet and made me dance.
Then he entered my very heart — and made me his own.
I cannot fathom this wonder.

These births and deaths — I gave them no thought.
I lay entangled with women, drowning in the flood of lips and sweet talk,
wandering the world as a madman.
He — the ocean of qualities, himself beyond all qualities and marks —
came together with Uma, approached,
drew close to me,
and claimed me as his own.
I cannot fathom this wonder.

The two karmas that swing this body-life back and forth — he severed them.
He who cannot be known even by those who strain to know through sound —
that one gave me consciousness, made me into light.
He cut the grip of every bond.
By his supreme, exalted, immeasurable compassion —

In this wretched life I lay around like a mangy, worm-infested dog —
running desires' errands, serving the wrong people, complying with any whim,
wandering on and on.
In this world, not even Vishnu and Brahma can reach his fragrant flower anklet-feet —
yet Father showed those very feet to me.
Even me — he took in as his own.
I cannot fathom this wonder.
