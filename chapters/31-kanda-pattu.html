Kanda Pattu

கண்ட பத்து
Chapter 31 · Composed at Thillai
← Ch. 30 All Chapters Ch. 32 →

Kanda Pattu (The Ten of Beholding) carries the subtitle "Vision of the Dance" (Niruttha Daricanam). It marks a turning point: every previous chapter asked, longed, or cried out; every verse here closes with "I beheld!" — past tense, a completed vision. The setting shifts from Thirupperunthurai to Thillai (Chidambaram), the great hall of Shiva's cosmic dance. What is beheld is not merely Shiva's form but the entire sequence of actions by which he claimed the poet — entering the womb before form existed, clearing the mind, severing karma, binding him with the rope of the mind to his feet, making others call him a madman. Each verse names what Shiva did and closes: I saw it happen. It was at Thillai.

At the time before form could be discerned, he entered within,
settled in my mind, established himself as thought, entered my flesh,
and with compassion claimed me — he who dwells at Tirutturuti, the sweet state of Shiva:
I the dog-servant, with longing, beheld it at adorned Thillai!

Staggering in the whirlpool of caste, clan, and birth —

and became flawless nectar: I beheld him at Thillai where he sports!

Birth completely turned away, disease and old age gone along with all their kin —

settled in the Tiruchitrambalam of Thillai city surrounded by dense groves,
with Brahmins and celestials bowing in worship — I beheld him!

Without devotion or fitness from me, he graciously severed my animal bondage,
caused people to say "this one is a madman" without my moving away,
and used the strong rope called "mind" to bind me to his holy feet:
the sport of that Master — I beheld it at shining Thillai!
