Ataikkala Pattu

அடைக்கலப் பத்து
Chapter 24 · Composed at Thirupperunthurai
← Ch. 23 All Chapters Ch. 25 →

Ataikkala Pattu (The Ten of Seeking Refuge) is a sustained act of complete surrender. In each of the ten verses, Manikkavasagar ends with the same cry: "O my Master, this servant takes refuge in you." The chapter opens with a devastating self-portrait — a body full of worms, a filthy mind, useless learning, no wisdom — while the true devotees with ripened hearts have already reached liberation. Verse after verse the poet confesses his weakness: he is drowning in the sea of suffering, battered by desire, lying exhausted in a body of darkness, unable even to know the path to worship. Each confession ends in the same act — complete surrender to Shiva as the only refuge.

The devotees with ripened hearts — clustered like rich lotuses at your sacred red feet and settled there in peace — have gone ahead to liberation. I, the sinner, remain: this body a wretched cage full of worms, my learning useless, my heart empty of wisdom, my mind filthy. O my Master, this servant takes refuge in you.

O One who bears — through your greatness alone — my wretchedness, for I do nothing but despicable things! O One who wears the serpent as your ornament, O One who holds the surging Ganga within your matted locks, O One who by your divine grace severs my cycle of rebirth at the root — O my Master, this servant takes refuge in you.

O Great Lord who severs my rebirth at the root and bestows the great divine ecstasy! O Lord of wondrous skill who comes within my very mind! O rare Great Lord who stood unknowable even to lotus-born Brahma and great Vishnu — O my Master, this servant takes refuge in you.

In the pouring storm-flood of sorrow, the devotees who took your ankle-feet as their raft descended into it and crossed over — they have ascended to heaven. But I am in the jaws of the sea of suffering — sucked into the whirlpool, battered by waves of alluring women, struck by the sharks of desire. I am being destroyed. O my Master, this servant takes refuge in you.

Caught in the trap of those with curly braids, forgetting your greatness, I lie exhausted in this body filled with darkness. O One who shares half your form with the one whose gaze is like a startled deer with wide dark eyes — O Great Lord of the celestials — please bestow your grace. O my Master, this servant takes refuge in you.

Broken by the power of those with collyrium-darkened eyes, I have gone as slack as curd spread flat in a pot. O blessed one — when will I come, on what day will I bow at your wide lotus feet? I who carry such heavy karma — O great Father, O my Master, this servant takes refuge in you.

Caught in the net of those with lightning eyes and swaying waists, I wander about in misery. O One with gracious eyes — please enter me so I may wander no more, let nectar flow sweetly in my eyes, and show compassion for my faults. O my Master, this servant takes refuge in you.

O One who shares half your form with the one whose eyes are like slivers of unripe mango — my mouth calls out to your lotus feet, my mouth bows in worship, yet I do not know your will.
