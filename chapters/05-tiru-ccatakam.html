Chapter 5 · Composed at Thiruperundurai
← Ch. 4 All Chapters Ch. 6 →

A hundred verses tracing ten inner stages of the soul’s ascent: from Realization of Truth (mey uNardhal) through Instruction, Piercing the Veil, Self-Purification, Reciprocal Exchange, Experiential Purity, Lament of Compassion, Weeping in Bliss, Supreme Bliss, to Transcendent Bliss (Ananda Adheeda) — Manikkavasagar pours out his unworthiness, his surrender, and his ultimate dissolution into Shiva at Thiruperundurai.

My body bristles with goosebumps, I tremble and quake — my hands placed on my head at your fragrant locks, tears welling over, my heart burning within. Falsehood forsaken, I cry “Victory! Victory! Praise!” — these hands I will not release. O Lord, look upon me and take me!

Though my family be ruined, I will not covet the life of Indra, Vishnu, or Brahma — I will keep no company except your devotees, even if it means entering hell. O Lord, if I may dwell by your holy grace, I will not disdain it. Our Supreme One, I hold no other deity in my heart but you!

Melting within, meditating on the feet of that Supreme One, that Father, that Lord — others see me with bewildered mind and say “this one is mad.” They wander town to town, each speaking only what their own mind holds. When, I wonder, shall I ever die to this world?
8

Those who feasted at Daksha’s sacrifice, then feared the poison and cried “Alas, Father!” with hasty offerings — those we call ours. But to count themselves among the Three alongside our Lord, to rule heaven and lord over earth, proudly proclaiming themselves gods — what sin, what relentless pride is theirs!

I performed no austerity; I did not worship faithfully with cool flowers — I was born in vain, heavy with karma. Your devotees attain the glory of Shiva, but I have not reached that fortune, O Supreme Ancestor. Grant me even rebirth itself — grant me anything — that I may reach your sacred feet!
10

The devotees who worship your feet without fail, offering many chosen flowers, hold in their hearts the certainty that all they pray for shall be theirs — O Thief who does not hide away, grant to me too that same unbroken love for your long locks, so I may praise you wholly and completely!

Brahma — who created Vishnu who claimed to see all — once bowed his crown and searched everywhere with lush flowers, yet found you beyond reach. But here you are on this very side, our Lord! You play your drama in the cremation forest alongside a donkey, wearing tiger skin, goal-less, wandering free with your datura sprig!
12

Wind, fire, water, earth, sky — pervading all — across all passing time and whatever comes after: you are the guardian who will protect us, dissolving the fierce karma that I, your servant, have accumulated through all these wandering ages!

The very breath is our Lord, garlanded with the cool great moon, Lord of the celestials — Shiva is our Lord, who claimed me despite my smallness. In this manner — He is our Lord; I am his servant — may the way of knowing the Lord of the world be proclaimed to all!

I am not fit to enter among your devotees — I am a flawed gem. Yet fitting it was that you claimed me! You raise even the most lowly on high and make the very celestials bow, O Elder Brother, O Ambrosia. Truly laughable, my Lord, this drama you have performed with me!

Play-acting like your devotees, I find myself rushing headlong toward liberation! O Gem-Mountain gleaming with gold, grant that unbroken love for you may stand within me and melt me from inside — O our Lord, grant this!

I do not fear birth; I am not afraid of death. I do not desire heaven or care to rule the earth. O Shiva of honey-dripping konRai flowers, our Lord, our Deer — I yearn again and again, asking: when, oh when, shall I receive your grace?

I yearn to see your lotus feet — this dog-servant, sitting here, offering no fine flowers, not praising you till the tongue grows callused. O you who bent your perfect golden bow, if you do not bestow your grace-ambrosia on me, I shall suffer — this lonely one — what else shall become of me?

I do not melt inwardly for your sacred feet; I feel no love; I string no flower garlands; I neither praise nor speak your glory; O King of the divine ones, I sweep not your temple, anoint it not, nor dance within it — yet I rush headlong toward death itself. O you who are known to the wise!

Grant liberation and grace — Praise — O True One among your truth-filled ones!
