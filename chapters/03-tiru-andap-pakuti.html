Tiru-Andap-Pakuti

திருவண்டப் பகுதி
Chapter 3 · Composed at Tillai (Chidambaram)
← Ch. 2 All Chapters Ch. 4 →

Tiru-Andap-Pakuti — 'The Sacred Cosmic Section' — is the third chapter of Thiruvasagam, composed in agaval metre at Tillai (Chidambaram). The poem opens with a breathtaking expansion of scale: the cosmos unfolds in concentric layers — worlds within worlds, oceans within oceans — all generated by the playful Lord whose creative energy sets everything spinning. The middle section is a sustained 'Behold!' sequence, accumulating epithets for Shiva — dancer, destroyer, sustainer, the sacred bull, the crescent moon, the river — in cascading lists that enact the very abundance they describe. The final movement turns to intimate address, drawing the cosmic hymn back to the personal: the same Lord who spins galaxies has reached down to the poet, and his grace falls like monsoon rain washing away all bondage. The poem closes with a series of vaazhka ('long may he live') refrains, returning to celebration after the vast cosmic survey.
