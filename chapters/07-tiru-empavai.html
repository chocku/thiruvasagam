Thiruvempavai

திருவெம்பாவை
Chapter 7 · Composed at Thiruvannamalai (Annamalai)
← Ch. 6 All Chapters Ch. 8 →

Thiruvempavai is 20 verses in the Empavai tradition — young women rise before dawn in the Margazhi month to bathe in the sacred pond and worship Shiva. Manikkavasagar casts the verses as a scene where a group moves from door to door, waking sleeping friends and calling them to join. Each verse ends with the refrain "Elor Empavai" — "Come, girls, to our vow." The girls tease, cajole, and praise their friends while declaring their devotion to Shiva as the formless Light without beginning or end, the fire-dancer of Thillai, and the Lord of Annamalai who is at once male, female, sky, and earth. The chapter moves from gentle humor to ecstatic praise and closes in a sustained litany of worship. It is one of the most beloved chapters in Thiruvasagam.

We sing of the great radiant Light that has no beginning and no end. Even hearing us, O beautiful-eyed one, do you keep sleeping? Are your ears made of stone? The sound of worship for Mahadeva's long-reaching feet rang out through the streets. The moment some people heard it, they sobbed and sobbed, lost all sense of their bodies, and rolled off their fragrant beds. She just lay there, unable to do anything at all. What a wonder! What a wonder! Is this how our friend behaves? Come, girls, to our vow.

Our love is bound to the Supreme Light. Day and night we speak of him — yet you have set your heart on this fragrant bed! O jeweled one, shame on you! Are these things nothing to you? Is this any way to spend your time? Even the gods in heaven approach his lotus feet with awe. He is the Lord of glory, the Lord of Shiva's realm, who has come graciously to give us those feet in the sacred hall of Thillai. We are the ones who love him — but who are we, really? Come, girls, to our vow.

O girl with teeth white as pearls! You always come running and call out: "My Father! My Bliss! My Nectar!" — the words overflow sweetly from your mouth. Come now, open your door! You have devotion, you have been servants of Isa for a long time, you are our good companions. Would it not be good to take us new servants too, and free us from our worthlessness? Does anyone not know how loving you are? Would those with beautiful hearts not sing of our Shiva? This much is all we ask. Come, girls, to our vow.

O girl with a bright sesame-flower smile! Has dawn broken yet? Have all the bright-voiced parrot-women arrived? We will count and tell you how things stand. Until then, do not waste your time with useless sleep. He is the one remedy for the heavens, the beloved object of the Vedas, the one who is sweet to the eye. We cannot sing of him, melt with feeling, and have our hearts dissolve inward all by ourselves. You must come. If after counting everyone is here, then go back to sleep. Come, girls, to our vow.

You go around boasting "We know him just as well" — that mountain whom even Vishnu cannot know and even four-faced Brahma cannot see. You deceiver with your honey-and-milk mouth, open your door! He cannot be known by the earth, the sky, or anything else. We sing of his beautiful form and of his faultless nature of taking us as his own. We call out "Shiva! Shiva!" — and you still will not wake! O girl with cardamom-scented hair, is this your way? Come, girls, to our vow.

O deer-like one! Yesterday, without any shame, you said: "Tomorrow I will come and wake you all myself." Tell me — where did you go? Has dawn even broken yet? He cannot be known by sky, earth, or anything else — yet he himself came, took us under his care, and claimed us as his own. Open your mouth for those who have come singing his heavenly long-reaching feet. Let your very flesh melt. Sing of the King — it is good for you, for us, and for all others. Come, girls, to our vow.

"The child in your own hands is your refuge" — out of our fear we renew that ancient saying and address it to you, our great Lord. Listen to what we ask: may our breasts not touch the shoulders of any who are not your devotees. May our hands serve no one but you. Day and night, may our eyes see nothing else. If our King grants us this, what beauty or sun do we need beyond it? Come, girls, to our vow.
