Thiruppadai Atchi

திருப்படை ஆட்சி
Chapter 49 · Composed at Thillai (Chidambaram)
← Ch. 48 All Chapters Ch. 50 →

Thiruppadai Atchi (The Sacred Army's Rule) is a chapter of eight long verses, each structured as a litany of conditional promises. Every verse lists seven or eight things that will come to pass — karmas dissolved, senses transformed, bliss attained, enemies routed — and then closes with a single condition: "IF" the Lord comes, enters, reveals himself, or rules. The structure enacts the devotee's whole longing: everything is possible, everything is ready to break open — and there is only one thing needed. The chapter is one of the most formally ambitious in the collection.

Will not both my eyes rejoice at the sight of his sacred feet? Will not my life — spent in the ways of the world's beauties — reach its end? Will not the endless cycle of being born into this earth and forgetting come to its close? Will we not worship those two lotus feet that even Vishnu could not fathom? Will we not be immersed in songs and dances that bring ever-deepening rapture? Will we not sing the Thiruppadai Atchi — hymns of the Lord of the glorious Pandya land? Will not some rare, heaven-gladdening wonder come to be revealed? — IF the hunter who cast his grace-net comes and reveals himself!

Will not one resound with one, and five vibrate with five in perfect harmony? Will not grace flow like the mother-cow who rises thinking of her calf? Will not the beginningless causal qualities that bind the soul be severed? Will not the trembling agitation of "this is good, this is bad" dissolve? Will we not all go and join the company of his devotees? Will not the supreme nectar, overflowing with undying love, be attained? — IF the Bull-rider, my Master who rules me, enters within me!

Will not the distorted qualities of bondage be torn away and overthrown? Will not the supreme nectar, born from the mind's own sacred contemplation, come to be? Will not the boundless, undivided Infinite be realized within us? Will not the supreme radiance — the primordial primal wonder — draw near? Will not the troubles stirred by the red-lipped beauties of this world scatter away? Will not these fish-like eyes revel in beholding his sacred body? Will not the sorrows of birth — the suffering of this illusory world — depart? — IF my own Master, the Lord Isa, appears before me!

Will not the bliss of resting upon his jeweled chest come to be? Will we not bathe sweetly today in the boundless great ocean of compassion? Will not the divine gem-resonance reverberate and reach within my heart? Will not the Lord's sacred ash come to adorn us every day? Will not my service stand foremost among his steadfast devotees? Will we not worship those lotus feet that even the great Vedas cannot fathom? Will not the beautiful red water-lilies shower down upon my head? — IF I receive the grace of my great Lord, the gracious Isa who holds me!

Will not the delusion fashioned by maya upon this earth be cut away? Will we not worship those flower-feet that even the celestials cannot fathom? Will not the turmoil that has darkened all the blind stretches of time cease? Will not the hearts of his devoted lovers exult with joy today? Will not the discord of clinging to identity — woman, man, or neither — dissolve? Will not the countless errors of nameless births be resolved? Will not innumerable attainments come and find me? — IF I am graced by the coming of my great Lord, the gracious Isa who governs me!

Will not the longing of the lightning-slender devoted ones be revealed? Will not joy overflow in the humming, rising sound of the veena? Will not the pure jewel-sound that rises beyond all words be savored? Will not the divine radiance that has settled within my heart continue to rise? Will not the Supreme beyond the Supreme — pervading all and coming without end — be realized? Will not supreme experiences, never before known, spread and arise within us? Will not the love-longing of the fair-browed devoted ones come to fruition today? Will not this very thing be the excellent treasure that even the celestials cannot know? Will not the boundless eight divine qualities be attained? — IF we are graced by the coming of the moon-crested jewel to rule us!

Will not the resounding sound of gathered conch shells rise and flourish? Will not the clinging qualities of caste distinction be shaken from us at last? Will not the illusion of the mind's endless "this is good, that is good" be subdued? Will not all desire become simply this: to be the servant of his servants' servants? Will not the minds of the bright-eyed devoted ones revel in him? Will not the Shiva-experiences of the excellent devotees be spread and shared? Will not the supreme radiance — the nectar welling up and filling everywhere — be attained? — IF I receive the grace of the endless Vedic Lord who rules me, deigning to come!
