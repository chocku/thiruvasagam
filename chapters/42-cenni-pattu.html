Senni Pathu

சென்னிப் பத்து
Chapter 42 · Composed at Thiruperundurai
WonderPraise
← Ch. 41 All Chapters Ch. 43 →

Senni Pathu (The Song of the Crown) is a ten-verse meditation on bowing the head. The word "senni" (சென்னி) means head, crown, or summit. Each verse describes Shiva's cosmic attributes — the Supreme of the Supreme, the eight-formed one, the dancer of Thillai, the one who shares his form with Uma — and closes with the same refrain: "may our head dwell at his great-lotus sacred red feet and radiate / blossom / glow / shine." The variation in the final word of the refrain — radiate, blossom, glow, shine — signals that the act of bowing is not static but alive, yielding new light at each touching. The chapter ends with a direct call to devotees to come and bow so that their bonds may be severed. The theology is compressed: when the head — the summit of the self — is placed at the feet of God, the whole self is transformed.

God of gods · the true servant (Shiva who serves by taking lowly forms) · lord of southern Thiruperundurai · unknowable even by the three (Brahma, Vishnu, Rudra) — who is the primal source — the embodiment of bliss · by whoever — except those with love / other than devotees — the radiant lotus-light — cannot be known · at the pure great-lotus sacred red feet — our head — abiding there — may it radiate!

God of gods — the true servant — lord of southern Thiruperundurai,
the embodiment of bliss, primal source unknowable even by the three,
the radiant lotus-light who can be known by none but those who love him:
may our head dwell at his pure, great-lotus sacred red feet and radiate!

The eight-formed beautiful one — sweet nectar, the very flood of bliss —
the refined true lord of Shivaloka, servant of southern Thiruperundurai,
the beautiful one who placed the honey-haired young goddess on one half of his body:
may our head dwell at his round, great-lotus sacred red feet and blossom!

may our head dwell at his surging, great-lotus sacred red feet and glow!

Surrounded by devotees, the Supreme of the Supreme — as if descending to survey this earth —
surrounded by Siddhas, Lord Shiva who dances in the ancient city of Thillai:
that sweet trickster who came and entered our homes, claimed us, and took our service:
may our head dwell at his placed great-lotus sacred red feet and blossom!
