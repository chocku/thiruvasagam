Tiru-Kazukkunnra-Patikam

திருக்கழுக்குன்றப் பதிகம்
Chapter 30 · Composed at Thirukazhukunram
WonderGratitude
← Ch. 29 All Chapters Ch. 31 →

Tiru-Kazukkunnra-Patikam (The Hymn of Tirukazhukunram) carries the subtitle "Vision of the Guru" (Guru Daricanam). Tirukazhukunram — "the sacred hill of the vulture-eagle" — is a site near Kanchipuram where Shiva appeared directly before the saint. This is the only chapter in the Thiruvasagam composed at a location other than Thirupperunthurai, and also the shortest, with seven verses. Each verse traces a detail of the poet's wretchedness or wandering — he dried his tears when he should have wept, he strayed and did not approach, he sank in the ocean of shame — and closes with the same disclosure: "you came and revealed yourself at Kazhukunram." The connection with Perunthurai persists as an epithet throughout, but the place of vision is Kazhukunram. Shiva did not wait for the saint to arrive at the right place; he came.

O great Lord of Perunthurai without quarrel — for those who speak your names,
incomparable joy comes and all suffering is wiped away, O our Lord.
Not letting the imperishable seed bear fruit again, when my karma was exhausted:
your innumerable beautiful forms — you came and revealed them at Kazhukunram.

O great madman of Perunthurai who carried clay like a ball of rice —
I who did not come in proper conduct, the worthless one who did not approach you:
O Refined One! O One of Shivaloka!
Even me — worse than a little dog, the fierce sinner — to take me in,
you came and revealed yourself at Kazhukunram.

I who dried my tears — whose impurities you destroyed at Perunthurai —
I who went astray, the wretched sinner, not knowing what lay ahead:
your two radiant sacred feet had nowhere to rest — I was troubled.
Without trouble yourself, you came and revealed yourself at Kazhukunram.

Wearing an unwearable love and praising fittingly each day,
overwhelmed by unattainable shame, sinking in the middle of the ocean —
grasping and steering the great vessel of Perunthurai that needs no governing:
your otherwise invisible divine form — you came and revealed at Kazhukunram.

O cloud of Perunthurai — Varaha of beautiful form, essence of all qualities!
O crown-jewel set in my mind that knows no propriety:
taking the earth as witness, longing and longing for you to come —
meditating on you, you came and revealed yourself at Kazhukunram.

O great flood of Perunthurai who gave undivided grace —
you who caused many wrongs to be spoken of me before strangers — what did you do to me?
"Death, death — let corruption end! Singular refuge — refuge!" — loving you thus,
meditating on you, you came and revealed yourself at Kazhukunram.

O Lord who granted the eight qualities to the sixty-four attendant women!
Though I was sunk in the ancient fierce karma of the three impurities that cause delusion —
you severed my bondage, drew me in, gave me your pure lotus feet,
and, overwhelming me before devotees, you came and revealed yourself at Kazhukunram.
