Thirupporr Cunnam

திருப்பொற்சுண்ணம்
Chapter 9 · Composed at Thillai (Chidambaram)
← Ch. 8 All Chapters Ch. 10 →

Thirupporr Cunnam ("The Sacred Golden Paste") is 20 verses in Aruceer Aasiriya Virutham metre, composed at Thillai. It is a joyful work-song cast as women pounding sacred spiced paste as an offering to Shiva. Each verse ends with the refrain "āda porr cuNNam idiththu nāmē" — "let us pound the golden sacred paste and dance!" The verses call on goddesses, devotees, and the whole world to join in, invoke Shiva's great deeds and attributes, and celebrate his grace with exuberance. The chapter radiates the ecstasy of communal devotion — the world itself becomes the mortar, Mount Meru the pestle, and truth the turmeric being pounded.

1

lift garlands of fine pearl-jasmine · place the sprouted pot, incense, and good lamps · Sakthi, Somi, and Earth-goddess · let Sarasvati join them and sing long life · let Siddhi, Gauri, and Parvati · let Ganga come and wave the fly-whisks · singing the ammanai of our Father the Lord of Aiyanaru · let us pound the golden sacred paste and dance!

Lift garlands of fine pearl-jasmine. Place the sprouted pot, incense, and good lamps. Let Sakthi, Somi, the Earth-goddess, and Sarasvati sing long-life hymns. Let Siddhi, Gauri, Parvati, and Ganga come and wave the fly-whisks. Singing the ammanai of our Father, the Lord of Aiyanaru — let us pound the golden sacred paste and dance!
2

for our Lord of the long flower-adorned matted locks · we must pound the auspicious golden paste · weeping like spots on a mango leaf · come, all of you, come and sing together · call out! let no devotee stand outside · bow down, worship our King, our Dancer · as the Goddess and he come to claim us · let us pound the golden-red sacred paste!

For our Lord of the long flower-adorned matted locks, we must pound the auspicious golden paste. Come weeping like spots on a mango leaf — all of you, come and sing together. Call out! Let no devotee stand outside. Bow down, worship our King, our Dancer. As the Goddess and he himself come to claim us — let us pound the golden-red sacred paste!

smear sacred ash beautifully · scatter pure gold and spread riches · plant Indra's wish-fulfilling tree everywhere · set up beautiful lamps and raise the banner · Lord of Indra, master of Brahma · Lord of Vishnu, father of the good-speared Murugan · who is always husband of Uma who rules all · let us pound the fitting golden sacred paste!

Smear sacred ash beautifully, scatter pure gold and spread riches. Plant Indra's Karpaga tree everywhere, set up beautiful lamps and raise the banner. He is Lord of Indra, master of Brahma, Lord of Vishnu, father of the good-speared Murugan — who is always the husband of Uma who rules all. Let us pound the golden sacred paste that befits him!
4

adorn all the pestles with gold · decorate the dark mortars with bamboo · "may the devoted servants · stand and flourish forever!" — blessing thus · singing of the glorious red-gold temple of Sri Ekamban at Kanchi praised across all lands · standing and tearing away the binding karma · singing, let us pound the golden sacred paste!

Adorn all the pestles with gold, decorate the dark-stained mortars with bamboo. Bless them saying "May all devoted servants stand and flourish forever!" Singing of the glorious red-gold temple of Sri Ekamban at Kanchi, celebrated in praise across all lands — standing and tearing away the binding karma, singing, let us pound the golden sacred paste!
5

அறுகெடுப்பார் அயனும்அரியும் அன்றிமற்றிந்திரனோட அமரர் · நறுமுறு தேவர்கணங்கள் எல்லாம் நம்மிற்பின் பல்லதெடுக்க வொட்டோம் · செறிவுடை மும்மதில் எய்தவில்லி திருவேகம்பன் செம்பொற்கோயில்பாடி · முறுவற்செவ்வாயினீர் முக்கணப்பற்கு ஆடப்பொற்சுண்ணம் இடித்துநாமே

Brahma and Vishnu who scatter sacred grass · Indra and all the gods besides · all the fragrant masses of divine beings · we will not let any one outpace us · singing of the three-walled temple · the Archer who shot the three forts — Sri Ekamban at Kanchi · O you with smiling red mouths · for the three-eyed Father · let us pound the golden sacred paste!

Brahma and Vishnu who scatter sacred grass, Indra and all the gods — all the fragrant masses of divine beings — we will not let any one of them outpace us! Singing of the three-walled temple of the Archer who shot the three forts, the glorious red-gold shrine of Sri Ekamban at Kanchi — O you with smiling red mouths, for the three-eyed Father, let us pound the golden sacred paste!
6

great ones have come to wield many pestles · the whole world is not enough for the mortar · devotees have come and stood to make the paste · the worlds are not enough even to behold it · claiming us devotees so that we might flourish · giving daily-blooming flower-feet as our crown · singing and singing with joy of the Lord who is nephew to the mountains · let us pound the golden paste!

Great ones have come to wield many pestles — the whole world is not enough for the mortar, they say. Devotees have come and stood to make the paste — the worlds are not enough even to behold it, they say. He claimed us so that we might flourish and gave us daily-blooming flower-feet as our crown. Singing and singing with joy of the Lord who is nephew to the mountains — let us pound the golden sacred paste!

As our bangles and shoulders ring and ring, as crowds of devotees rise and cheer and cheer — as the people of the land cheer us and we cheer for them — for Uma with soft anklet-feet, who is his half, our Paramatman — for the King who is like a great golden mountain, let us pound the golden sacred paste and dance!
8

O lovely wide-eyed tender women · as your lined bangles ring and your full breasts heave · as sacred ash gleams thick and bright on your shoulders · saying and saying "Grant me this, O my Lord!" · showing the daily-blooming flower-feet he granted · us who were lower than dogs in this life · singing and singing of how he claimed us · let us pound the golden sacred paste and dance!

O lovely wide-eyed tender women — as your lined bangles ring and your full breasts heave, as the sacred ash on your shoulders gleams thick and bright, saying and saying "Grant me this, O my Lord!" — he shows the daily-blooming flower-feet he granted to us who were lower than dogs in this life. Singing and singing of how he claimed us, let us pound the golden sacred paste and dance!

making the whole world the mortar · planting great Mount Meru as the pestle · filling it full with "truth" as the turmeric · the glorious Lord of Perunthurai in the great south · singing and singing of his red sacred feet · holding the golden pestle in the right hand · for our Lord, the one who adorns lovely Thillai · let us pound the golden sacred paste and dance!

Making the whole world the mortar and planting great Mount Meru as the pestle — filling it full with Truth as the turmeric — he is the glorious Lord of Perunthurai in the great south. Singing and singing of his red sacred feet, holding the golden pestle in the right hand — for our Lord who adorns lovely Thillai, let us pound the golden sacred paste and dance!
