Neettal Vinnappam

நீத்தல் விண்ணப்பம்
Chapter 6 · Composed at Thiruuthara Koshamankai
← Ch. 5 All Chapters Ch. 7 →

Neettal Vinnappam ("Petition for Renunciation") is 50 verses in Kattalaik Kalitthurai metre, all composed at Thiruuthara Koshamankai. Each verse is a direct plea to Shiva — the King of that sacred town — not to abandon the poet to the world. Every verse carries the refrain viTutikaNTāy ("see that you release me") and closes by addressing the Lord as King of Uttara Koshamangai. The poet compares himself to a moth plunging into flame, a small fish gasping when water dries up, an ant inside a burning stick, a creeper without a branch — each image a plea to be freed from the five senses and the weight of karma, and taken into the grace of Shiva.

1

கடையவனேனைக் கருணையினால் கலந்து ஆண்டுகொண்ட · விடையவனே விட்டிடுதி கண்டாய் விறல் வேங்கையின் தோல் · உடையவனே மன்னும் உத்தரகோசமங்கைக்கு அரசே · சடையவனே தளர்ந்தேன் எம்பிரான் என்னைத் தாங்கிக்கொள்ளே

O Lord who rides the bull — by your grace you merged with me and claimed me, the most wretched of the wretched. See that you release me. O wearer of the fierce tiger skin, eternal King of Uttara Koshamangai, O matted-haired Lord — I have grown faint. My Lord, hold me up.
2

Women with full breasts pressed close, with kovvai-red lips — I cannot let go of them. Yet see that you release me. In your noble service I am neither fully within nor fully without, O King of Uttara Koshamangai. What was your reason for seeing and claiming even this deceiver?
3

I am like a tree on a riverbank, taking root deeper in the five senses through dark-eyed women and being washed away. See that you release me. O eternal dweller of the glorious town, King of Uttara Koshamangai — O one who shares your body with jewel-breasted Uma, O you who nurture me.
4

Even as you reached out your growing grace to draw me in, I slipped away and wander here. See that you release me. O King of Uttara Koshamangai whose tall crown bears a single shoot of the white moon — you are a rich resplendent flame, glowing like refined gold and flashing like lightning.
5

Like a moth drawn into blazing flame, I have fallen day after day among beguiling women. See that you release me. O King of Uttara Koshamangai whose flower-crown is alive with fragrant bees — when you stood on the path to offer me the nectar of your grace, I refused.
6

I refused out of ignorance of your grace, O my jewel. Do not despise and cast me away. Punish me for my accumulated karma, then claim me, O King of Uttara Koshamangai. Do not the great ones endure even the falsehoods of little dogs?
7

பொய்யவனேனைப் பொருள் என ஆண்டு ஒன்று பொத்திக்கொண்ட · மெய்யவனே விட்டிடுதி கண்டாய் விடம்உண் மிடற்று · மையவனே மன்னும் உத்தரகோசமங்கைக்கு அரசே · செய்யவனே சிவனே சிறியேன் பவம் தீர்ப்பவனே

O True One — you claimed me, this lying wretch, as if I were something of worth, and held me close. Do not cast me away. O blue-throated one who drank poison, eternal King of Uttara Koshamangai — O ruddy Shiva, O you who end the cycle of birth for this small one.
8

தீர்க்கின்றவாறு என் பிழையை நின் சீரருள் என்கொல் என்று · வேர்க்கின்ற என்னை விடுதி கண்டாய் விரவார் வெருவ · ஆர்க்கின்ற தார்விடை உத்தரகோசமங்கைக்கு அரசே · ஈர்க்கின்ற அஞ்சொடு அச்சம் வினையேனை இருதலையே

"How will your glorious grace clear away my faults?" — wondering this, I sweat with anguish. See that you release me. O Lord of the garland-decked bull who makes enemies tremble, King of Uttara Koshamangai — the five senses pull at me from one end, and fear from the other.
9

Like an ant trapped inside a two-ended burning stick, I am separated from you and my mind wanders everywhere. See that you release me. O sole master of the vast three worlds, eternal King of Uttara Koshamangai — you who shine holding the three-bladed trident in your right hand.
10

I was granted entry to your radiant feet, I was granted the release of the body — yet here I grow faint and wither. See that you release me. O King of Uttara Koshamangai whose flowering grove resounds with the hum of bees seeking nectar — you burned the three cities that turned against you with your powerful strong bow.
11

The five senses turned against me and deceived me — I have been separated from your jewel-like flower-feet. See that you release me. You are present in the mind of me, this karma-burdened one, only as long as longing wells up. O eternal King of Uttara Koshamangai — O great noble one with a golden body that shows its light even through the sacred ash.
12

பழிப்பில் நின்பாதப் பழந்தொழும்பு எய்தி விழப்பழித்து · விழித்திருந்தேனை விடுதிகண்டாய் வெண்மணிப்பணிலம் · கொழித்து மந்தாரம் மந்தாகினி நுந்தும் பந்தப்பெருமை · தழிச்சிறை நீரில் பிறைக்கலஞ்சேர் தரு தாரவனே

pazhippil nin paatham pazhanthozhumbу eyithi vizhap pazhitthu · vizhiththirunthenaai viduti kandaay venmaaNip paNilam · kozhitthu mantaaram mantaakini nuntum bantapperumay · thazhichsiRai neeril piRaik kalanjseer tharu thaaravanee

your blameless feet · ancient service having reached · stumbling and reproaching myself · me who lay wide-eyed unable to act · see you release me · white jewel-conches · swelling up · mandaram flowers · Mandakini river pushing them · greatness of this bond · in clear cool water · a crescent-moon boat · O one who wears the garland that brings union

Having reached the ancient service at your blameless feet, I stumbled and lay there wide-eyed. See that you release me. O one who wears the garland that brings union — the Mandakini river piles up white jewel-conches and pushes mandaram flowers, and in those clear waters rides a crescent-moon boat of your greatness.
48

தாரகைபோலும் தலைத்தலை மாலைத் தழலரப்பூண் · வீர என்தன்னை விடுதிகண்டாய் விடில் என்னை மிக்கார் · ஆர் அடியான்என்னின் உத்தரகோசமங்கைக் கரசின் · சீரடியாரடியான் என்று நின்னைச் சிரிப்பிப்பனே

tharakai pooLum thalai thalai maalaith thazhalarap puuN · veera en thannaai viduti kandaay vidin ennaai mikkaar · aar adiyaan ennin uttarakoshamangaikku arasin · seer adiyaar adiyaan endRu ninnaich chirippibbane

like stars piled garland upon garland · wearing fire-serpents as ornament · O hero · see you release me · if you abandon me · who is greater than me · if they say "I am your servant" · of the King of Uttara Koshamangai · "I am servant of the glorious devotees" · saying this · I will make you laugh

O hero who wears fire-serpent garlands piled like stars upon stars — see that you release me. If you abandon me and someone asks "Who is greater among your servants?" — I will say: "I am the servant of the glorious devotees of the King of Uttara Koshamangai" — and make you laugh.
49

சிரிப்பிப்பின் சீறும் பிழைப்பையும் தொழும்பையும் ஈசற்கென்று · விரப்பிப்பன் என்னை விடுதிகண்டாய் விடின் வெங்கரியின் · உரிப்பிச்சன் தோலுடைப்பிச்சன் நஞ்சூண்பிச்சன் ஊர்ச்சுடுகாட்டு · எரிப்பிச்சன் என்னையும் ஆளுடைப்பிச்சன் என்றேசுவனே

chirippibin cheerRum pizhaibbaiyum thozhumbbaiyum eesaRkendRu · virappibban ennaai viduti kandaay vidin vengkariyim · uribbichsam tooludaip bichsan nanjuuN bichsan uurchudukaaT Tu · eripbichsam ennaiyum aaLudai bichsan endRu yechuvanee

if I make you laugh · even my scathing fault · and my service · "for Isha" · I will offer them up · see you release me · if you let go · the fierce elephant · the lunatic who skinned it · madman who wears the hide · madman who ate poison · town's cremation ground · madman who burns there · "the madman who also rules over me" · saying this they will mock you

If I make you laugh, I will offer even my scathing fault and my service up to Isha. See that you release me. If you let me go, people will mock you saying: "That lunatic who skinned the fierce elephant, who wears the hide, who ate poison, who burns in the town's cremation ground — that madman has me under his rule too!"
50

ஏசினும் யானுன்னை ஏத்தினும் என்பிழைக்கே குழைந்து · வேசறுவேனை விடுதிகண்டாய் செம்பவள வெற்பின் · தேகடையாய் என்னை ஆளுடையாய் சிற்றுயிர்க்கு இரங்கிக் · காய்சின ஆலம்உண்டாய் அமுதுண்ணக் கடையவனே

esinNum yaan unnaai ethinNum en pizhaikkee kuzhainthu · veesaRuvenaai viduti kandaay sembavala veRpin · the kadaiyaay ennaai aaLudaiyaay chiR uyirkku irangiik · kaaysina aalam uNdaay amuthuNNak kadaiyvanee

whether I mock you or praise you · melting away in my own faults · me who am in distress · see you release me · O one of the red-coral mountain · O churner of the divine nectar · O one who rules over me · having compassion for small souls · you ate the burning halahala poison · so that others might drink nectar · O you who churned the ocean

Whether I mock you or praise you, I melt away in my own faults and am in distress. See that you release me. O one of the red-coral mountain, O churner of the divine, O one who rules over me — you had compassion for small souls and ate the burning poison so that others might drink nectar. O you who churned the ocean.
