Tiru Pulampal

திருப்புலம்பல்
Chapter 39 · Composed at Thiruvarur
← Ch. 38 All Chapters Ch. 40 →

Tiru Pulampal (The Sacred Lament) is a very short chapter — only three verses — but each is a complete surrender. The first declares that Manikkavasagar will praise nothing other than Shiva's sacred feet, the path unknown even to Brahma and Vishnu. The second lists Shiva's forms and names and affirms that he knows no other support. The third — the most intimate — renounces everything: kin, village, fame, and learning itself. All he wants is to melt and dissolve at Shiva's feet like a calf straining toward its mother. The chapter's subtitle, "the maturity of the bliss of Shiva" (சிவானந்த முதிர்வு), signals that these are words spoken from the far shore of devotion, not its beginning.

O one who walks a path unknown to lotus-born Brahma and the great Vishnu! O one smeared with white ash who shares your form with the lady whose breasts are adorned with red kino blossoms! O Lord of Thiruvarur, ringed by tall ramparts! I, this servant, will praise nothing else but your flowery sacred feet — nothing whatsoever.

O one with matted locks! O dancer in fire! O one who bears the gleaming three-pronged trident! O Supreme Light! O Pasupati! O one with the young white bull! O Lord of Thirupperunthurai ringed by spreading groves! I, this servant — O Lord who possesses all — I know no true support other than you.
3

I want no kinfolk. I want no town, no name. I want no learned company. What is to be learned — now enough of it. O Dancer who is settled and dwelling in Kuttalam! At your resounding sacred feet, I want only to melt and dissolve — like the mind of a calf that has tasted its mother's milk.
