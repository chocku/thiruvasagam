Thirupalliyezhuchchi

திருப்பள்ளியெழுச்சி
Chapter 20 · Composed at Thirupperunthurai (Āvudaiyār Kōvil)
LongingWonder
← Ch. 19 All Chapters Ch. 21 →

This is the sacred dawn-hymn of the Thiruvasagam — the song sung at the break of day to rouse Shiva from sleep at the holy shrine of Thirupperunthurai. As Manikkavasagar stands at the temple in the pre-dawn darkness, the world is waking around him: cuckoos call, roosters crow, conches sound, stars fade, and the sun rises. Around him worshippers have gathered on every side — veena players, Veda chanters, those carrying flower garlands, those weeping, those trembling with devotion, those raising folded hands to their foreheads in prayer. Each of the ten stanzas ends with the same yearning refrain: "O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!" The hymn is also read as a deeper allegory: just as Shiva is called to arise from physical sleep, so too is the soul called to awaken from the darkness of ignorance into the light of divine knowledge. The final stanzas grow in personal intensity, recounting how Shiva appeared as a Brahmin, showed His sacred feet and His temple, and claimed the poet as His own.

Homage! O Essence, the very source and foundation of my life — dawn has broken! Taking matching pairs of flowers for your sacred lotus feet, lifting them up before your sacred face, receiving the beautiful grace-blooming smile you pour on us — let us worship your sacred feet! O great Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai, surrounded by cool fields where muddy-petalled lotuses open their eyes! You who bear the exalted bull-flag on high! You who own me! O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!

Arunan = Aruna(dawn-charioteer/red-of-dawn) · Intiran-thisai = Indra's-direction(east) · anuginan = has-approached · iruḷ = darkness · pooy = gone · aganrthathu = has-dispersed · utayam = sunrise · nin = your · malar = blooming · thirumugaththin = of-the-sacred-face · Karunayin = of-compassion · suuriyan = sun · ezha-ezha = rising-and-rising · nayanam = eye · kati = fragrant/closed-bud · malar = flower · malara = to-bloom · matru = moreover · annal = lord · angkan = beautiful-eyed · aam = is · Thiraḷ-nirai = clustered-rows · aruphatham = six-footed(bees) · muraḷvana = humming · ivaiyoor = listen-to-these · AruḷNithi = treasure-of-grace · thara = to-give · varum = coming · aanantham = bliss · malaiyee = O-mountain · alai = waves · katalee = O-ocean · paḷḷi-ezhunthu-aruḷaayee = please-arise!

Aruna, the red charioteer of dawn, has approached the eastern direction! Darkness has gone and dispersed. As the sun of Your compassion rises from Your blooming sacred face, the fragrant eye-like flowers bloom fully open — and our beautiful-eyed Lord appears! Rows of clustering bees hum their six-footed song — listen to them! O great Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai — O mountain of bliss that comes to grant the treasure of grace! O ocean of waves! Please arise from your sacred rest!

Kuuvina-puung-kuyil · kuuvina-koozhi · kurukugaḷ-iyampina · iyampina-shangam · Oovina-thaarakai-oḷi · oḷi-utayaththu-oruppatugintrathu · viruppoṭu-namakku · Theva! nal-sheṛi-kazhal-taaḷinai-kaattaay · Thirupperunthurai-urai-Siva-perumānee · Yaavarum-arivariyaay · emakkuzhiyaay · emm-perumaan-paḷḷi-ezhunthu-aruḷaayee

The flower-cuckoos have called! The roosters have called! The egrets have cried out! The conches have sounded! The starlight has faded; the light of sunrise is gathering together. O God, with love, show us your pair of sacred feet adorned with beautiful dense anklets! O great Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai — hard for all the world to know, yet easy and near for us! O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!
4

இன்னிசை வீணையர் யாழினர் ஒருபால் · இருக்கொடு தோத்திரம் இயம்பினர் ஒருபால் · துன்னிய பிணைமலர்க் கையினர் ஒருபால் · தொழுகையர் அழுகையர் துவள்கையர் ஒருபால் · சென்னியில் அஞ்சலி கூப்பினர் ஒருபால் · திருப்பெருந் துறையுறை சிவபெருமானே · என்னையும் ஆண்டுகொண்டு இன்னருள் புரியும் · எம்பெருமான் பள்ளி எழுந்தருளாயே

Innisai = sweet-music · viinayar = veena-players · yaazhinnar = harp-players · orupaal = one-side · irukku = Rig-Veda · oṭu = with · thoththiram = praise-hymns · iyampinam = reciting · orupaal = one-side · Thunniya = closely-joined · pinai = bound · malar = flower · kaiyinar = those-with-flowers-in-hands · orupaal = one-side · thozhugaiyar = worshipping-ones · azhugaiyar = weeping-ones · thuvaḷgaiyar = trembling-ones · orupaal = one-side · Shenniyil = on-the-head · anjali = joined-hands-in-prayer · kuuppinar = those-raising · orupaal = one-side · Ennai-yum = me-too · aantu-kontu = having-claimed-as-servant · inn-aruḷ = sweet-grace · puriyum = bestowing · paḷḷi-ezhunthu-aruḷaayee = please-arise!

On one side, sweet-music players with veenas and harps! On one side, those reciting the Rig Veda and praise hymns! On one side, those holding tightly bound flower-garlands in their hands! On one side, worshipping ones, weeping ones, trembling ones! On one side, those raising their joined hands to their foreheads in prayer! O great Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai — claim even me as your servant and bestow your sweet grace! O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!

"பூதங்கள் தோறும் நின்றாய்!" எனின் அல்லால் · "போக்கிலன் வரவிலன்" என நினைப் புலவோர் · கீதங்கள் பாடுதல் ஆடுதல் அல்லால் · கேட்டறியோம் உனைக் கண்டறிவாரைச் · சீதங்கொள் வயல்திருப் பெருந்துறை மன்னா · சிந்தனைக்கும் அரியாய் · எங்கண் முன்வந்து · ஏதங்கள் அறுத்தெம்மை ஆண்டருள் புரியும் · எம்பெருமான் பள்ளி எழுந்தருளாயே

Puuthangaḷ = elements(five-elements) · thorrum = in-every · nindraay = You-who-stand · enin-allaal = unless-saying · Pookkillan = without-going · varavillan = without-coming · ena = saying · ninai = thinking-of · pulavoor = learned-poets/scholars · Kiithangaḷ = songs · paatuthal = singing · aatuthal = dancing · allaal = other-than · keettariyoom = we-have-not-heard · unai = you · kantariyaaraich = those-who-have-seen-and-known · Siitham = coolness · koḷ = taking · vayal = fields · mannaa = O-King · shinthanaikku-m = even-to-contemplation · ariyaay = hard-to-attain · engaṇ-mun = before-us · vanthu = coming · Eethangaḷ = faults/defects · aruttu = cutting · emmai = us · aantu = ruling · aruḷ-puriyum = bestowing-grace · paḷḷi-ezhunthu-aruḷaayee = please-arise!

Unless one says "You who stand in every element" or "He who neither goes nor comes" — we have not heard of anyone who has truly seen and known You, beyond those learned ones who sing songs and dance in Your honor. O King of Perunthurai with cool fields — even thought cannot reach You! Come before us, cut away our faults, claim us as Your servants and bestow Your grace! O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!

பப்பற வீட்டிருந் துணரும் நின் அடியார் · பந்தனை வந்தறுத்தார் அவர் பலரும் · மைப்புறு கண்ணியர் மானுடத்தியல்பின் · வணங்குகின்றார் அணங்கின் மணவாளா · செப்புறு கமலம் கண் மலருந்தண்வயல் சூழ் · திருப்பெருந் துறையுறை சிவபெருமானே · இப்பிறப் பறுத்தெமை ஆண்டருள் புரியும் · எம்பெருமான் பள்ளி எழுந்தருளாயே

Many of Your devotees, realizing liberation that spreads in all directions, have come and cut their bonds. Women with kohl-lined eyes worship You in the human way — O bridegroom of the goddess Parvati! O great Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai, surrounded by cool fields where golden-cupped lotuses bloom open! Cut away this birth and claim us as your own, bestowing your grace! O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!

அதுபழச் சுவையென அமுதென · அறிதற்கு அரிதென எளிதென · அமரரும் அறியார் · இதுஅவன் திருவுரு இவன் அவன் எனவே · எங்களை ஆண்டுகொண்டு இங்கெழுந்தருளும் · மதுவளர் பொழில்திரு உத்தரகோச மங்கையுள்ளாய் · திருப்பெருந்துறை மன்னா · எது எமைப் பணிகொள்ளும் ஆறு? அது கேட்போம் · எம்பெருமான் பள்ளி எழுந்தருளாயே

His nature — whether it is like the taste of ripe fruit, or nectar, whether it is hard to know or easy — even the gods of heaven do not truly know. Yet declaring "This is His sacred form, this very one is He," He has drawn us in as His own and is graciously present here. O You who dwell in Uttara Koshamangai of honey-filled groves! O King of Thirupperunthurai! In what way will You take us as your servants? We wish to hear that! O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!

Munthiya = prior/earlier · mudal = beginning · natu = middle · irudhiyum = and-end · aannaay = You-became · muuvarum = the-three(Brahma-Vishnu-Rudra) · arigiliaar = cannot-know · yaavar = who · matru = else · arivaar = will-know · Panthanai = ball(tender-fingers) · viraḷiyum = she-with-ball-like-fingers(Parvati) · niiyum = and-You · nin-atiyaar = Your-devotees · pazhangg-kutil = old/ancient-huts · thorrum = in-every-one · ezhunthu-aruḷiya = who-graciously-entered · paraanee = O-Supreme-One · Shem-tazhal = red-flame · purai = resembling · thirumeeeni = sacred-body · kaatti = showing · kooyilum = temple-too · kaatti = showing · Anthannan = Brahmin · aavathum = becoming-too · kaatti = showing · vanthu = coming · aantaay = claimed-as-servant · Aaramuthee = O-sweet-ambrosia · paḷḷi-ezhunthu-aruḷaayee = please-arise!

You became the beginning, the middle, and the end! The three — Brahma, Vishnu, and Rudra — cannot know You. Who else can know You? O Supreme One, You and Your ball-fingered Parvati graciously entered every ancient hut of Your devotees! You showed Your sacred body blazing like red fire. You showed Your temple at Thirupperunthurai. You showed Yourself appearing as a Brahmin — and came and claimed me as Your servant! O sweet ambrosia, please arise from your sacred rest!
9

விண்ணகத் தேவரும் நண்ணவும் மாட்டா · விழுப் பொருளே · உனதொழில் படியோங்கள் · மண்ணகத்தே வந்து வாழச் செய்தானே · வண்திருப் பெருந்துறையாய் · வழி அடியோம் · கண்ணகதே நின்று களிதரு தேனே · கடலமுதே · கரும்பே · விரும்படியார் · எண்ணகத்தாய் · உலகுக்கு உயிரானாய் · எம்பெருமான் பள்ளி எழுந்தருளாயே

Vinnagam = heaven · thevaram = gods · nannnavum = to-approach/reach · maattaa = unable · vizhup-poruḷee = O-precious-essence · unathozhil = your-work/service · patiyoonggaḷ = we-who-follow-your-way · Mannagaththee = on-the-earth · vanthu = coming · vaazha = to-live · sheythaanee = O-one-who-made · van = generous · Thirupperunthuraiyaay = O-Lord-of-Perunthurai · vazhi = path · atiyoom = we-are-servants · Kannagathee = within-the-eye · nintru = standing · kaḷi = joy · tharu = giving · theenee = O-honey · katalamuthee = O-ocean-nectar · karumpee = O-sugarcane · virumpatiyaar = devoted-ones-who-cherish · Ennagaththaay = O-one-dwelling-in-thought/mind · ulagukku = to-the-world · uyiraaaanaay = O-one-who-became-life/soul · paḷḷi-ezhunthu-aruḷaayee = please-arise!

O precious Essence whom even the gods of heaven cannot approach! We are those who walk in Your service. O generous Lord of Thirupperunthurai — You caused us to come to earth and live! We are servants on Your path. O honey that stands within the very eye and gives joy! O nectar of the ocean! O sugarcane! O one who dwells in the minds of Your devoted ones who cherish You! O one who is the very life and soul of all the worlds! O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!
10

"புவனியிற் போய் பிறவாமையின் நாள் நாம் · போக்குகின்றோம் அவமே · இந்தப் பூமி · சிவன் உய்யக் கொள்கின்ற வாறென்று" நோக்கித் · திருப்பெருந் துறையுறைவாய் · திருமாலாம் · அவன் விருப்பெய்தவும் மலரவன் ஆசைப் · படவும் நின் அலர்ந்த மெய்க் கருணையும் நீயும் · அவனியிற் புகுந்தெமை ஆட்கொள்ள வல்லாய் · ஆரமுதே · பள்ளி எழுந்தருளாயே

Puvaniir-poooy-piravaamayin-naaḷ-naam · pookkugintroom-avamee · inthap-puumi · Sivan-uyyak-koḷgindra-vaaru-endru-nookki · Thirupperunthurai-uraivay · ThiruMaaḷ-aam · Avan-viruppeythavum-malaravan-aasai-p · patavum-nin-alarntha-meyk-karunaiyum-niiyum · Avaniyir-pugunthu-emai-aatkkoḷḷa-vallaay · Aaramuthee · paḷḷi-ezhunthu-aruḷaayee

Puvaniyir = in-the-world · poooy = going · piravaamai = not-being-reborn(spiritually) · naaḷ = days · naam = we · pookkugintroom = are-spending/wasting · avamee = in-vain · inthap-puumi = this-earth · Sivan = Shiva · uyyak-koḷgindra = saves/redeems · vaaru = the-manner · endru = saying · nookki = seeing/realizing · Thirupperunthurai-uraivay = O-one-who-dwells-at-Perunthurai · ThiruMaaḷ = Vishnu · aam = is · Avan = he(Vishnu) · viruppu = desire · eythavum = attaining · malaravan = Brahma(lotus-born) · aasai = desire · patavum = having · nin = your · alarntha = bloomed/open · meyk-karunaiyum = true-compassion · niiyum = and-You · Avaniyir = on-earth · pugunthu = entering · emai = us · aatkoḷḷa = to-claim-as-servants · vallaay = capable · Aaramuthee = O-sweet-ambrosia · paḷḷi-ezhunthu-aruḷaayee = please-arise!

"We spend our days in this world in vain, going through life without achieving true rebirth in You" — seeing this, and seeing the way in which Shiva redeems — O Lord who dwells at Thirupperunthurai! Even Vishnu longs for You; even Brahma the lotus-born burns with desire for You. Your true open compassion and You Yourself entered this earth and are fully capable of claiming us as Your servants! O sweet ambrosia, please arise from your sacred rest!
