Acca Pattu

அச்சப் பத்து
Chapter 35 · Composed at Thillai (Chidambaram)
Wonder
← Ch. 34 All Chapters Ch. 36 →

Acca Pattu (The Song of Fear) is Manikkavasagar's paradox of courage and dread. He fears nothing the world can throw at him — not the cobra in the anthill, not the ocean of karma, not the spear, not a woman's glance, not disease or death, not fire, not falling mountains, not thunder, not even Yama's fury. But let him see someone who does not love Shiva — who thinks there is another god, who feels no revulsion at false deities, who has no love, no compassion, no sacred ash, no devotion, no wisdom — and he trembles. Each verse ends with the same refrain: "அம்ம! நாம் அஞ்சுமாறே!" — "Ah, how we tremble!" The fear is not selfish dread but the anguish of compassion: the sight of a soul untouched by Shiva is the one thing Manikkavasagar cannot face with equanimity. The ten verses catalog different failings — lovelessness, heartlessness, the absence of sacred ash, ignorance, timidity before Shiva's worship — while circling back each time to the same shudder of grief and wonder.

I do not fear the gleaming serpent in the anthill; I do not fear the "truth" of false men. But when I see those who — instead of drawing near the feet of our Lord with flowing braided locks and the eye on his forehead — think "there is another god" and have not surrendered to our great Lord — ah, how we tremble!

I do not tremble when desire comes; even if the ocean of karma engulfs me, I do not fear. But when I see those who — beside the sacred form of our Lord and Master, whom even Brahma and Vishnu could not fathom — feel no revulsion at the thought of "what other god is there?" — ah, how we tremble!

I do not fear the sharp spear; I do not fear the sidelong glance of bangle-wearing women. But when I see those who — unable to gaze at him who dances in the Stage until their very bones melt, unable to praise my incomparable jewel and sweetly drink in his grace — those without love — ah, how we tremble!

I do not fear the speech of parrot-like women; I do not fear their crafty smiles. But when I see those who — instead of drawing near the feet of the Vedic one adorned with white sacred ash, with tears flooding their eyes, worshipping, weeping, hearts melting — are without compassion — ah, how we tremble!

Even if all diseases come upon me, I do not fear; I do not fear birth and death together. But when I see those who — instead of sinking among the devotees of him who wears the shining crescent, extolling the sacred red feet that even the great Vishnu who split the solid earth could not see — do not wear the white sacred ash — ah, how we tremble!

I do not fear the blazing fire; even if mountains roll and tumble, I do not fear. But when I see those who — instead of praising the lotus feet of our Father, the Bull-rider with sacred ash on his shoulders, who is beyond the reach of words, adorning him with broad flowers and yearning — are not his servants — ah, how we tremble!

I do not fear unbounded blame; I do not fear death itself.
