Aticiya Pattu

அதிசயப் பத்து
Chapter 26 · Composed at Thirupperunthurai
Wonder
← Ch. 25 All Chapters Ch. 27 →

Aticiya Pattu (The Ten of Wonder) carries the subtitle "Nature of Liberation" (Mutti Ilakkanam). Each verse catalogues the speaker's unworthiness in specific terms — his attachment to women and wealth, his ignorance of righteous paths, his readiness for hell — and closes with the same astonishing refrain: a named epithet of Shiva "claimed me and united me with his devotees — what a wonder we have witnessed!" The wonder deepens with each verse as the disproportion grows more extreme: it is not the worthy but the thoroughly worthless who are seized and raised. The final verse shifts register: Shiva destroys the three luminous forts with fury and red fire, gracing the true path and removing the false one — the cosmic act of liberation and the personal act of liberation are the same wonder.

Without melting in my heart — not for heaps of treasure, not for the radiance of rubies —
I was wasting away in the company of firm-breasted women.
That Father — of incomparable, simile-transcending bright-flowered holy feet —
claimed me and united me with his devotees. What a wonder we have witnessed!

I think of nothing righteous; I do not join those who do.
In fault alone I wander, born and dying again and again.
Yet the Primal One who stands eternal, joined with Uma as his half,
called me his devotee, claimed me, and united me with his devotees. What a wonder we have witnessed!

When my former fierce karma fell away, our Father of three eyes —
difficult for all to know, yet easy for his devotees —
who set the young crescent moon in his matted crown that surpasses gold:
that Mother took me in and united me with his devotees. What a wonder we have witnessed!

Hear the reason the world calls me a madman:
not knowing the way to go and meet his holy grace,
I was set to die and fall into dreadful hell.
That Lord claimed me and united me with his devotees. What a wonder we have witnessed!

I never went to join those who sing his praises; I never gathered flowers to offer.
Standing in the way of fragrant-haired women, I was ruining myself.
Our Lord who dances with fire at midnight, his blazing matted hair flaring bright —
that Serpent-wearer made me his own and united me with his devotees. What a wonder we have witnessed!

I never recited his holy five letters — such was my wretchedness.
I never drew near those learned in scripture, not desiring good deeds.
Set to be born and die on this earth and turn to dust —
that Great One drew me in and united me with his devotees. What a wonder we have witnessed!

This false dwelling — rotten flesh-walls, worm-ridden, festering, oozing —
I took as real and stood caught in the whirlpool-sea of suffering.
The full radiance of pearl, great jewel, ruby, diamond, and coral:
that Lord claimed me and united me with his devotees. What a wonder we have witnessed!

That Maker claimed me and united me with his devotees. What a wonder we have witnessed!

Like fragrance rising from a fine flower — the true substance within this body —
the Supreme Being is beyond all grasp and fixed place.
Not seeking that Being, not clearing myself of fools' words, just enjoying whatever came —
that Endless One claimed me and united me with his devotees. What a wonder we have witnessed!
