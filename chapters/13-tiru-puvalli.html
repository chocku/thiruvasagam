Thirupuuvalli

திருப்பூவல்லி
Chapter 13 · Composed at Thillai (Chidambaram)
← Ch. 12 All Chapters Ch. 14 →

Thiruppuuvalli ("The Sacred Flower-Creeper") is a flower-gathering folk-song in 20 verses in Naladith Tharavu Kocchagak Kalippaa metre, composed at Thillai. Women go out to gather flowers and sing Shiva's praises along the way, each verse closing with the refrain "shall we pluck the flower-creeper?" The chapter's stated theme is "removing the victory of Maya" — Shiva cuts the bonds of kinship and old karma, destroys the cycle of rebirth, and enters the devotee from within. The verses move across several of his sacred sites — Thillai, Idaimarudu, Perunthurai, Ekambaram, Uttara Koshamangai, and Madurai — praising the grace he showed at each. The register is warm and intimate: he placed his feet on the poet's head, entered the body within, counted even the most wretched as worthy, and broke ancient karma at its very root. Grand mythological acts — burning the three cities, eating the halahala, humbling Ravana and Daksha, the Madurai miracle of the pitta-meal — appear woven into the same gentle song-gathering tone.

the matchless sacred feet · on my head · the very moment he placed them · all the kinships that were my support · every one of them · I renounced and left behind · in the hall of Thillai where the waters never cease · who dances · the ferryman who carries across · singing his greatness · shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

The very moment he placed his matchless sacred feet on my head, I renounced and left behind every kinship that had been my support. Singing the greatness of him who dances in the hall of Thillai where the waters never cease — the ferryman who carries us across — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
2

My father, my mother, all kinship, and everything else — the Lord of the Pandya country who severed all my bonds and claimed me. Praising the hollow where the honey of bliss dwells in that Idaimarudu — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
3

He counted even us — more wretched than dogs — as worthy. Our great Lord, whose compassion exceeds even a mother's — whose feet sever the illusory cycle of birth — singing how he ground my powerful karma to dust at its very mouth — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
4

without worshipping · the king of the melodious shrine of Thillai · being counted / being set right · Daksha · Surya · Bhrigu · Moon · Fire · by the sky-reaching Bhuta army · Veerabhadra · how they were wounded · singing · shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

Without worshipping the king of the melodious shrine of Thillai — Daksha, Surya, Bhrigu, the Moon, and Fire were all brought to account and wounded by his sky-reaching Bhuta army and Veerabhadra. Singing of how they were wounded — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
5

The great Lord Shiva with honey-roaming konrai flowers adorning his matted locks — he searched through my flesh, sought me out, and entered within, before the eyes of the world. As I danced and cried out — for the king of the sky-dwellers who practices the dance — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
6

for the three fire-gods · having compassion · graciously bestowing grace · the three skulls · perishing · graciously furrowing the sacred brow · becoming all three forms · the One who is hard to comprehend · how he burned the three cities · shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

Having compassion for the three fire-gods and graciously giving his grace, graciously furrowing the sacred brow to bring the three skulls to ruin — becoming all three forms — he is the One who is hard to comprehend. Singing how he burned the three cities — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
7

வணங்கத் தலை வைத்து வார்கழல்வாய் வாழ்த்தவைத்து · இணங்கித்தன் சீரடியார் கூட்டமும்வைத் தெம்பெருமான் · அணங்கொடணிதில்லை அம்பலத்தே ஆடுகின்ற · குணங் கூரப் பாடிநாம் பூவல்லி கொய்யாமோ

he placed the head · to bow · placed the mouth at his long anklets · to sing praise · harmonized with his excellent devotees · placed us in their company · our great Lord · with the goddess · beautiful Thillai · in the hall · who dances · singing his qualities deep and full · shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

He placed the head to bow, placed the mouth to sing praise at his long anklets, and placed us in the company of his excellent devotees — our great Lord who dances in beautiful Thillai with the goddess. Singing his qualities deep and full — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
8

நெறிசெய் தருளித்தன் சீரடியார் பொன்னடிக்கே · குறிசெய்து கொண்டென்னை ஆண்டபிரான் குணம்பரவி · முறிசெய்து நம்மை முழுதுடற்றும் பழவினையைக் · கிறிசெய்த வாபாடிப் பூவல்லி கொய்யாமோ

neRiseyth aRuLiththan siirradiyaar ponnnadikkee · kuRiseyththu koNdennnai aaNdapiraan kuNamparavi · muRiseyththu nammai muzhuthudaRRum pazhavinnaiyk · kiRiseytha vaapaadip puuvalli koyyaamoo

graciously showing the path · to his excellent devotees' golden feet · marking me · taking me · the Lord who claimed me · singing his qualities · breaking / snapping · which afflicts us completely · old karma · how he tricked and outwitted it · singing · shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

He graciously showed the path, marked me for the golden feet of his excellent devotees, and claimed me as his Lord. Singing his qualities — how he tricked and snapped apart the old karma that had afflicted us completely — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
9

பன்னாட் பரவிப் பணிசெய்யப் பாதமலர் · என்ஆகம் துன்னவைத்த பெரியோன் எழிற்சுடராய்க் · கல்நா ருரித்தென்னை யாண்டுகொண்டான் கழலிணைகள் · பொன்னான வாபாடிப் பூவல்லி கொய்யாமோ

many days of praising and serving · the flower-feet · pressed to my body · the Great One · as a beautiful radiance · like stripping fiber from stone · claimed me as his servant · the pair of anklets · how they became golden · singing · shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

Many days of praising and serving — the Great One pressed his flower-feet to my body. Like stripping fiber from stone — impossible, yet as a beautiful radiance he claimed me as his servant. Singing how the pair of his anklets became golden — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
10

பேராசை யாமிந்தப் பிண்டமறப் பெருந்துறையான் · சீரார் திருவடி யென் தலைமேல் வைத்தபிரான் · காரார் கடல்நஞ்சை உண்டுகந்த காபாலி · போரார் புறம்பாடிப் பூவல்லி கொய்யாமோ

great longing · we · forgetting this body · the Lord of Perunthurai · the excellent sacred feet · on my head · the Lord who placed them · from the dark teeming sea · the poison · who joyfully ate · Kaapali (skull-bearer) · full of battle-glory · singing · shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

We, with great longing, forgetting this body — the Lord of Perunthurai who placed his excellent sacred feet on my head. Kaapali, the skull-bearer, who joyfully ate the poison from the dark teeming sea — singing his battle-glories — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
11

பாலும் அமுதமுந் தேனுடனாம் பராபரமாய்க் · கோலங் குளிர்ந்துள்ளங் கொண்டபிரான் குரைகழல்கள் · ஞாலம் பரவுவார் நன்னெறியாம் அந்நெறியே · போலும் புகழ்பாடிப் பூவல்லி கொய்யாமோ

milk and ambrosia and honey together · the supreme beyond the supreme · with a cool beautiful form · the Lord who has taken the heart · the resounding anklets · those who spread his praise throughout the world · that seems to be the good path itself · singing praise · shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

He is milk and ambrosia and honey together — the supreme beyond the supreme. With his cool and beautiful form, he has taken the heart. Those who spread the praise of his resounding anklets throughout the world — that seems to be the good path itself. Singing his praise — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
12

வானவன் மாலயன் மற்றுமுள்ள தேவர்கட்கும் · கோனவ னாய் நின்று கூடலிலாக் குணங்குறியோன் · ஆன நெடுங்கடல் ஆலாலம் அமுதுசெய்யப் · போனகம் ஆனவா பூவல்லி கொய்யாமோ

vaanavan maalayan maRRumuLLa theevarkattkkum · konavanaay ninRu kuudalillaak kuNangkuRiyoon · aana nedungkadal aalaalam amudhuseyya · poolnagam aanavaa puuvalli koyyaamoo

Standing as king of the sky gods, Vishnu, Brahma, and all the other gods — the one of incomparable qualities who bears the sacred mark. From the vast primordial sea he made the halahala poison into ambrosia. Singing how it became his meal — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
13

That day, under the banyan shade, he graciously gave the precious Vedas. Celestials and great sages stand and praise him fully every day. Singing of the golden pollen of the konrai flowers beautifully adorning his full-sounding anklets — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
14

படமாக என்னுள்ளே தன்னிணைப்போ தவையளித்திங் · கிடமாகக் கொண்டிருந் தேகம்பம் மேயபிரான் · தடமார் மதில்தில்லை அம்பலமே தானிடமா · நடமாடு மாபாடிப் பூவல்லி கொய்யாமோ

Spread like a painting within me, granting his own paired lotus feet, having made this place his dwelling — the Lord who abides at Ekambaram. Singing how he makes the broad-walled hall of Thillai his dancing ground — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

Agni, Surya, Ravana, Andhaka, Yama, red-eyed Vishnu, Brahma, Indra, the Moon, Daksha, and Bhrigu — all their dignity came undone before him. Singing his surging greatness — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
16

The fierce war-bull rider, the battle-bull hero of Sivapura — on earth in Madurai he graciously partook of the humble pitta-meal, and with the staff made the Pandya king bow down. Singing the song of that wound — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

Vishnu and Brahma — the foremost — together with the celestials and demons do not know his golden sacred feet, yet they praise him. He entered my body from within and claimed me. Singing of his shining ornament — the many-hooded serpent — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

the excellent sacred feet · the firm anklet · for the sound of the anklet-ringing · insatiable longing · I, the servant · heart rejoicing · the chariot-thronging street · the Lord of Perunthurai · performing the sacred dance · singing the great bliss · shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

My longing for the sound of the firm anklet on the excellent sacred feet is insatiable — my heart, your servant, rejoices. Singing the great bliss of the Lord of Perunthurai performing the sacred dance on the chariot-thronging street — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

the elephant · stripped · graciously wrapping himself in it · the Lord of Perunthurai · taking the form of a madman · in this world · also becomes a child · the complete root of liberation · Uttara Koshamangai's generous one · how he entered the mind · shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

The Lord of Perunthurai who graciously wraps himself in the stripped elephant skin — who takes the form of a madman in this world, who is also a child. Singing how the generous one, the complete root of liberation at Uttara Koshamangai, entered the mind — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?

Fully mounted on a horse, graciously entering the city of Madurai with his divine shining form — the Lord of Perunthurai coming as king for us, graciously wielding the faultless spear. Praising his flower-filled anklets — shall we pluck the flower-creeper?
