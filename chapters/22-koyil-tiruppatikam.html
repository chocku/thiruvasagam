Koyil Tiruppatikam

கோயில் திருப்பதிகம்
Chapter 22 · Composed at Thirupperunthurai
AnguishLonging
← Ch. 21 All Chapters Ch. 23 →

Koyil Tiruppatikam (The Shrine Tiruppatikam) is a deeply personal hymn to Shiva at Thirupperunthurai. Each of the ten verses closes with the refrain "Thiruperunthurai urai Sivane" (O Shiva who dwells at holy Thirupperunthurai). Where the cuckoo-song and dawn-hymn before it were full of tender longing, this chapter is quietly intense — Manikkavasagar addresses Shiva as the Supreme Light welling up within him, the Nectar that shut the door of the senses, the Fire hidden within water, the Hidden Form inside the five elements. He twice confesses that he has "no recompense" for what Shiva has given him. The chapter reaches its climax in verse 397 with a stunning paradox: "You gave your very Self — you took my very self — O Sankara, who are the clever ones who understand this exchange?"

Through love this servant's soul and body melted and dissolved into bliss — you gave me sweet grace beyond my reach, and I have nothing at all to offer in return. O Liberated One who pervades before, after, and all throughout — O Endless First Cause, O Sivaperumanae of holy Thirupperunthurai, O King of glorious Sivapuram!

O King, O Father of this servant among your devotees — you entered my soul and body, ripening me layer by layer, melting me, O true flame that drove away the darkness of falsehood! O clear sea of nectar that abides unbeaten by waves, O Shiva of holy Thirupperunthurai — O Awareness known only by transcending word and thought, please teach me the way to speak of you!

The great sages who realized you came to rest with the celestials — O Essence hard to discern even for understanding itself! O Peerless Soul of all living souls, O medicine that severs my cycle of birth — O pure expanse that shines clear within dense darkness, O Shiva of holy Thirupperunthurai — O Bliss beyond all qualities, now that I have drawn near to you, what want can remain?

O Fullness without lack, O Nectar without flaw, O Mountain of endless, rich flame — O King who came as both the Veda and its very meaning and abided within my mind — O Shiva of holy Thirupperunthurai who flows into thought like water that cannot be dammed — O Lord, you have taken my body as your abode; what more can I beg of you now?

O Light rising deep within my heart as I beg and beg until I melt — O one whose lotus-red feet gleam upon the crowns of the immortals, O Shiva of holy Thirupperunthurai — you are not the infinite sky, water, earth, fire, or wind, yet right there within them you are the Hidden Form — I rejoiced today, beholding you with my very eyes!

Today, gracing me and banishing darkness, you rose in my heart like the sun — I contemplated your nature that stands beyond thought, and there is truly nothing else at all but you. O Shiva of holy Thirupperunthurai, drawing near to whom one goes and goes, recedes to an atom, and becomes One — you are not even a single thing, yet without you nothing at all exists; who can truly know you?

thandhadu = what was given · un-thannai = your very self · kondadu = what was taken · en-thannai = my very self · Sangaraa = O Sankara · aarkolo = who indeed · sadurer = are the clever ones · andham = end · ondru-illa = without one · aanandham = bliss · petreen = I gained · yaadhu = what · nee = you · pettra = gained · dhondru = a thing · enpaal = from me · sindhayee = the mind itself · koyil-koNda = made as your temple · emberumaaan = O our Lord · Thiruperunthurai-urai = dwells at Thirupperunthurai · Sivane = O Shiva · enthaiye = O my Father · Eesha = O Lord · udal = body · idang = place · kondaai = you took · yaan = I · idharku = for this · ilan = do not have · oor = one · kaimmaare = recompense

You gave your very Self — you took my very self — O Sankara, who are the clever ones who understand this exchange? I gained endless, boundless bliss — but what did you gain from me in return? O our Lord who made my very mind your temple, O Shiva of holy Thirupperunthurai — O my Father, O Lord, you have taken my body as your abode; I have not a single thing to offer you in return!
