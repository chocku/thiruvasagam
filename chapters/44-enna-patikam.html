Enna Pathu

எண்ணப் பத்து
Chapter 44 · Composed at Thillai (Chidambaram)
LongingAnguish
← Ch. 43 All Chapters Ch. 45 →

Enna Pathu (The Song of Yearning) is a six-verse meditation in which the poet stands caught between awareness of his own unworthiness and the impossibility of living apart from God. The title comes from the word "enna" (எண்ண) — to think, to count, to yearn. Each verse accumulates confessions of failure — no devotion, no divine madness, no ecstatic speech, no proper vision — and then turns not to despair but to petition: "save me," "bestow your grace," "let not your grace be put to shame." The theology is of persisting love despite moral bankruptcy: the devotee cannot qualify for God but cannot function without him either, and that double bind is itself offered as a form of prayer.

1

பாருரு வாய பிறப்பறவேண்டும் · பத்திமை யும்பெற வேண்டும் · சீருரு வாய சிவபெரு மானே · செங் கமல மலர்போல் · ஆருரு வாய என் னார முதே · உன் அடியவர் தொகை நடுவே · ஓருருவாய நின் திருவருள் காட்டி · என்னையும் உய்யக்கொண் டருளே

taking earthly form / as one born into this world · I want my births to end / may my cycle of births cease · you of glorious form · O great Lord Shiva! · like a red lotus flower · you whose form is deep and full · O my most satisfying nectar! · amid the congregation of your devotees · appearing in one singular divine form / revealing your grace as one clear form · your sacred grace · grant that even I too may be saved

I want these earthly births to end; I want to receive true devotion as well.
O glorious-formed great Lord Shiva! — like a red lotus flower,
you whose deep form is my most satisfying nectar — amid the congregation of your devotees,
reveal yourself in one clear form, show your sacred grace, and save even me!
2

உரியேன் அல்லேன் உனக் கடிமை · உன்னைப் பிரிந்திங் கொருபொழுதும் · தரியேன் நாயேன் · இன்னதென்று அறியேன் · சங்கரா கருணையினாற் · பெரியோன் ஒருவன் கண்டுகொள் என்று · உன் பெய்கழல் அடிகாட்டி · பிரியேன் என்றென்று அருளிய · அருளும் பொய்யோ · எங்கள் பெருமானே

uriyeen-alleen-unak-kadimai · unnaip-pirindhink-koru-pozhudhum · thariyeen-naayeen · innadhenRu-aRiyeen · changkaraa-karuNaiyinaaR · periyoon-oruvan-kaNdukoL-enRu · un-peykazhal-adi-kaatti · piriyeen-enRenRu-aruLiya · aruLum-poiyyo · engaL-perumaane

I am not worthy / unfit · to be your servant · from you — separated here even for a moment · I cannot endure · I the dog / I the lowly one · what this is — I do not know · O Sankara (Shiva the Beneficent)! · through your compassion · "Let the Great One alone see to it" — saying this · your flower-adorned sacred feet — showing them · "I will not leave you" — saying again and again · the grace you then bestowed · is it a lie? · O our great Lord!

I am not worthy to be your servant — yet I cannot bear even a moment apart from you here.
I the dog: I know not what to call this state. O Sankara, through your compassion,
you said "Let the Great One alone see to it" and showed me your flower-adorned sacred feet,
crying again and again "I shall not leave you" — is that grace a lie, O our great Lord?
3

என்பே உருக நின் அருள் அளித்து · உன் இணைமலர் அடி காட்டி · முன்பே என்னை ஆண்டுகொண்ட · முனிவா · முனிவர் முழுமுதலே · இன்பே அருளி · எனையுருக்கி · உயிருண் கின்ற எம்மானே · நண்பே யருளாய் · என்னுயிர் நாதா · நின்னருள் நாணாமே

enbee-uruga-nin-aruL-aLithhu · un-iNai-malar-adi-kaatti · munbe-ennai-aaNdukoNda · munivaa · munivar-muzhumulale · inbe-aruLi · enai-urukkki · uyir-uN-kinRa-emmaane · naNbe-yaruLaay · en-nuyir-naathaa · nin-naruL-naaNaame

my very bones — making them melt · your grace — bestowing it · your pair of lotus feet — showing them · long before / from the beginning — me — who claimed as his own · O ascetic! O sage-like one! · O primal source of all the sages! · bestowing bliss itself · melting me · drinking / consuming my life-breath · O my Lord! · your friendship / loving companionship itself — bestow! (imperative) · O Lord of my life! · let not your grace be ashamed of me / may your grace not be put to shame

Making even my bones melt, you bestowed your grace and showed me your pair of lotus feet.
O ascetic! O primal source of all sages! — you who took me as yours from the very beginning,
bestowing bliss itself, melting me, drinking deep of my very life — O my Lord!
Grant me your very friendship, O Lord of my life — let not your grace be put to shame!
4

பத்தில னேனும் · பணிந்தில னேனும் · உன் உயர்ந்த பைங்கழல் காணப் · பித்தில னேனும் · பிதற்றில னேனும் · பிறப்பறுப்பாய் எம்பெருமானே · முத்தனை யானே · மணியனை யானே · முதல்வ னே முறை யோ என்று · எத்தனை யானும் · யான்தொடர்ந் துன்னை · இனிப்பிரிந் தாற்றேனே

paththila-nenum · paNinthila-nenum · un-uyarndha-paingkazhal-kaaNap · piththila-nenum · pithaRRila-nenum · piRappu-aRuppaay-em-perumaane · muththanai-yaane · maNiyanai-yaane · mudhalva-ne-muRai-yo-enRu · eththanai-yaaNum · yaan-thodarn-thunnai · ini-pirinnd-thaatReene

though I have no devotion · though I have not bowed / not submitted · to behold your lofty fresh-adorned sacred feet · though I have no divine madness · though I have not babbled in ecstasy · cut off my births! / sever the cycle of birth! (imperative) · O our great Lord! · calling you "O Pearl!" · calling you "O Gem!" · "O Primal One! — is this right? / is this proper?" · in whatever ways / however much · I have pursued you / followed after you · henceforth separated from you · I cannot endure / I cannot bear it

Though I have no devotion, though I have not bowed — longing to behold your lofty, fresh-adorned feet;
though I have no divine madness, though I have not babbled in ecstasy — end my births, O our great Lord!
Crying "O Pearl! O Gem! O Primal One — is this right?" —
however much I have pursued you, I cannot now endure separation from you!
5

காணும தொழிந்தேன் · நின் திருப்பாதம் கண்டு · கண் களிகூர · பேணும தொழிந்தேன் · பிதற்றம தொழிந்தேன் · பின்னை எம் பெருமானே · தாணுவே · அழிந்தேன் · நின்னினைந் துருகும் தன்மை · என் புன்மைகளால் · காணும தொழிந்தேன் · நீயினி வரினும் · காணவும் நாணுவனே

kaaNuma-thozhindeen · nin-thirup-paadham-kaNdu · kaN-kaLi-koora · peNuma-thozhindeen · pithaRRama-thozhindeen · pinnai-em-perumaane · thaaNuve · azhindeen · nin-ninaind-thurugum-thanmai · en-punmaigaLaal · kaaNuma-thozhindeen · nee-yini-varinum · kaaNauvum-naaNuvane

I have lost the vision / given up seeing · your sacred feet — beholding them · the eyes — that they may rejoice / revel · I have lost the desire to cherish / given up nurturing devotion · I have lost the ecstatic speech / given up babbling in devotion · after all this · O our great Lord! · O Thaanuva (Shiva — the Immovable Pillar)! · I am ruined / I am undone · the power to melt when thinking of you · through my own wretchedness / due to my smallness · I cannot look / I have lost even sight of you · even if you come now · to even look at you — I shall be ashamed / too ashamed to face you

I have lost the vision of your sacred feet — that sight which makes the very eyes rejoice.
I have lost the desire to cherish, lost the ecstatic speech — after all this, O my great Lord!
O Thaanuva! I am ruined — the power to melt in thought of you is gone, through my own wretchedness.
I have lost even the sight of you — and even if you come now, I shall be too ashamed to look!
6

பாற்றிரு நீற்று எம் பரமனை · பரங்கருணையோடு எதிர்த்து · தோற்றி · மெய்யடியார்க்கு அருட்டுரை யளிக்கும் · சோதியை · நீதியிலேன் · போற்றி என் அமுதே என நினைந்து · ஏத்தி புகழ்ந்து அழைத்து அலறி · என்னுள்ளே · ஆற்றுவனாக · உடையவனே · எனை ஆவ என்று · அருளாயே

paaR-thiru-neeRRu-em-paramanai · param-karuNaiyoodu-edhirththu · thooRRi · meyy-adiyaarkku-arud-durai-yaLikkum · choothiyai · neethi-yileen · pooRRi-en-amudhee-ena-ninaindhu · ethi-pugazhndhu-azhaitthu-alaRi · en-uLLe · aaRRuvanaaaga · udaiyavane · enai-aava-enRu · aruLaaye

glowing / shining — ash-smeared · our Supreme One (Paramam) · with supreme / transcendent compassion · appearing before / coming forward · manifesting · for the true devotees · the speech of grace — bestowing · that Radiance / that divine Light · I the unrighteous one / I without virtue · "Hail! — my nectar!" — thinking thus · praising · glorifying · calling · crying out within myself · so that I may become one who can endure / so I may be capable · O Lord who possesses all! · me · crying "Ah!" in tender pity · bestow your grace! (imperative)

You, the glowing, ash-smeared Supreme One — appearing with transcendent compassion,
manifesting before the true devotees, bestowing the speech of grace — O Radiance of Light!
I, the unrighteous one, thinking "Hail! My nectar!" — praising, glorifying, calling, crying aloud within —
O Lord of all: grant that I may endure! Cry "Ah!" for me in tender pity and bestow your grace!
