Kirtti-T-Tiru-Agaval

கீர்த்தித் திரு அகவல்
Chapter 2 · Composed at Tillai (Chidambaram)
← Ch. 1 All Chapters Ch. 3 →

Kirtti-t-Tiru-Agaval — 'The Sacred Praise-Hymn of Glory' — is the second chapter of Thiruvasagam, composed in agaval metre at Tillai (Chidambaram). It is a vast praise-hymn of 146 short verses cataloguing Shiva's gracious acts and disguises across dozens of sacred sites throughout Tamil Nadu — Thillai, Mahendra, Kalladam, Panchappalli, Madurai, Velamputtoor, Uttara Koshamangai, Ekambam, Tiruvarur, and many more. Each verse is a brief vignette: a grace bestowed, a devotee redeemed, a disguise assumed, a local legend honoured. There is no single refrain, but the hymn maintains an ecstatic forward motion as it sweeps through shrine after shrine. The poem opens at Thillai and circles south, east, and north before returning to its origin, closing with the image of devoted followers streaming behind Shiva as he makes his way back to Chidambaram — a procession of the redeemed.
