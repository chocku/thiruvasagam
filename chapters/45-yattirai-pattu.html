Yathirai Pathu

யாத்திரைப் பத்து
Chapter 45 · Composed at Thillai (Chidambaram)
← Ch. 44 All Chapters Ch. 46 →

Yathirai Pathu (The Song of the Pilgrimage) is a ten-verse rallying call to fellow devotees to set out for Shiva's sacred city without delay. The word "yathirai" (யாத்திரை) means journey or pilgrimage. Each verse picks up the imperative register — "cast away," "come," "be ready," "do not hesitate" — and builds toward the image of the caravan of devotees who journey together to the golden feet of Puyangan (the serpent-adorned Lord). The chapter is unusually collective and forward-moving: rather than a solo petition, it is a call to community, a spiritual rallying cry. The urgency is eschatological — "there is no more time," "the jeweled gate is opening" — and the chapter closes with a triple "alas!" at how rare true grace is in this world.

1

Our great serpent-adorned Lord — the flower-crowned King — drew in the lowly like us
through his unceasingly heart-entering, melting flood of grace.
O servants drawn in by the one who cried "Ah!" in tender love — come and gather together!
The time has come for us to go — behold! — leaving all falsehood to enter his sacred feet!
2

Do not plunge into the senses! Think deeply of the flower-adorned sacred feet of the great serpent-bearing Lord!
Let go of all excess — cast away whatever is unnecessary!
He who laughed and entered this world and took us in — as lowly as dogs —

3

They themselves are their own kin; they themselves hold their own fate.
"Who are we? What is ours? What is bondage? What illusion is this!" — when these dissolve —
fix your mind on the Lord's own will and set out with his ancient devotees.
Prepare to go — casting off all falsehood — to the golden feet of Puyangan who rules us!
4

O all of you who have become his servants — cast away your worldly games completely!
Come and reach his very feet — fix his sacred will in your hearts and remain!
He who will cast off the body's defilements and place us in the realm of Shiva —
let us enter the flower-adorned sacred feet of Puyangan whose body is smeared with sacred ash!
5

விடுமின் வெகுளி · வேட்கை நோய் · மிகவோர் காலம் இனி இல்லை · உடையான் அடிக்கீழ் · பெருஞ்சாத்தோடு · உடன் போவதற்கே ஒருப்படுமின் · அடைவோம் நாம் போய் · சிவபுரத்துள் · அணியார் கதவம் அடையாமே · புடைபட்டு உருகி · போற்றுவோம் · புயங்கன் ஆள்வான் புகழ்களையே

Cast away anger and the disease of desire — there is no more time!
Be ready to journey together with the great caravan to the feet of our Lord!
We shall go and reach Sivapuri — through its jeweled gate, unobstructed!
Melting completely in devotion, we shall praise the glories of Puyangan who rules us!
6

(imperative) · all suffering / afflictions · henceforth any obstacle · without it reaching us · gleaming / resplendent · full of glory · in Sivapuri · having gone / worshipping Shiva's feet · the devotees who dwell there · going before them / in front of · hearts melting · we shall stand!

Praise! Worship! Offer flowers! — fixing the very feet of Puyangan in your mind!
Scorn all suffering — let no obstacle come near us henceforth!
Having gone to the resplendent, glorious Sivapuri and worshipped Shiva's feet,
we shall stand before the devotees who dwell there, hearts melting in devotion!
7

Let those who stay, stay — in this impermanent world we shall not remain! We are going!
To the golden feet of Puyangan whose sacred body gleams like gold!
All of you — without delay, prepare yourselves to truly stand with him!
For if you hesitate and hang behind, the great Lord is hard to attain!
8

O those who have received the grace of dwelling inseparably in his supreme bliss —

At the very moment the jewel-adorned sacred gate of Sivapuri opens,
