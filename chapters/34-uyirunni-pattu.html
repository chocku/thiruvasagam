Uyirunni Pattu

உயிருண்ணிப் பத்து
Chapter 34 · Composed at Thirupperunthurai
WonderSurrender
← Ch. 33 All Chapters Ch. 35 →

Uyirunni Pattu (The Song of the Soul-Feeder) is a ten-verse hymn of joyous dissolution. "உயிருண்ணி" — the one who devours and absorbs the soul — is Manikkavasagar's name for Shiva in this chapter: he entered the poet's body, mingled with his soul, and will not leave. The opening refrain sets the register: "O Bull-rider, destroyer of karma, who dwells in Thirupperunthurai praised by eloquent bards — O sweet one, which day will I rejoice and feel exalted?" Each verse answers that question. Shiva set a throne for a dog who came to his feet, entered flesh and soul and heart, melted the marrow of the spine, and became the poet himself. The chapter is not anguish but wonder — each verse rings with "I do not know what he has done to me," not as complaint but as awe. By verse 511 the poet renounces everything — fame, wealth, earth, heaven, rebirth — and clings only to the feet of the Lord. The final verses overflow: what shall I call you — honey? Nectar? The poet knows himself only as a remnant, yet asks with quiet certainty: even though I am a sinner, may I not say to you — grant me your grace?

He set a throne here for a dog who would come to his feet — and I am that dog. He who dwells eternally in Thirupperunthurai, his matted hair filled with honey, entered this flesh-filled body, mingled with my very soul, and will not leave my heart. He has given us a wealth that even the celestial gods cannot fathom.

I do not know what "I" am; I cannot tell whether it is day or night. He who is beyond mind and words has taken this madman and made him even more mad. O Supreme Light! The ancient Lord who has the fierce great bull, who dwells in Thirupperunthurai — I do not know what he has done to me.

Are there others in this wide world whose karma has been destroyed as mine has? Tell me! He entered me himself, took me as his servant, and melted even the marrow of my spine. Then he entered further still — oh! The great Lord who dwells in Thirupperunthurai. He is in my mind, in the very eye of my eye — and I stand caught between two worlds.

You who have shed all attachments — grasp that one true attachment! If you would reach the good state of refuge, you who are lost, come running! Like ocean waves — cutting away the waves of impurity and turmoil — he entered my body and my soul and filled them so that he will not leave. I do not want fame; I do not want wealth. I do not want earth or heaven. I do not want to be born again among those who forever have no want of Shiva — I will not touch them. I have gone and joined the sacred feet of the eternal Lord of Thirupperunthurai. I have embraced him and will not go outside — I will not allow myself to stray outward ever again.

What shall I call you — honey? The nectar of the roaring ocean? I cannot bear it, O our Shiva! O rare medicine! O my King! You who dwell in Thirupperunthurai surrounded by fertile mud-filled fields — O Pure One adorned with sacred ash — it is I, and it is you!
