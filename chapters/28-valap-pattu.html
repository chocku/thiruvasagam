Valaap Pattu

வாழாப் பத்து
Chapter 28 · Composed at Thirupperunthurai
SurrenderLonging
← Ch. 27 All Chapters Ch. 29 →

Valaap Pattu (The Ten of "I Cannot Live") carries the subtitle "Means to Liberation" (Mutti Upaayam). The speaker is stripped of all alternatives — "save for you I have no other refuge, do you see?" — and makes a single demand: grace me by saying "come." Each verse invokes Shiva as King of Sivapura and as Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai, then presses a specific wound: the poet withers; he has forgotten the grace he was given; he does not know how to melt and live united; he refuses to worship anyone else. Some verses introduce Parvati — she of melody-voiced speech, cotton-soft feet, ball-playing fingers, faultless words — as the one who shares Shiva's form, making even her a reason why there is no other refuge. The chapter is among the most compressed acts of surrender in the Thiruvasagam: every verse is a direct address, every line a refusal to look elsewhere.

O our Supreme One who spreads as earth and sky — I have no other refuge, do you see?
O one resplendent with glory, King of Sivapura, Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai —
with whom should I grieve? To whom should I cry out and tell?
If you who claimed me have no grace, in this wide ocean world I cannot live, do you see?
Grace me by saying "come."

O great gem who took in the worthless one — me — I have no other refuge, do you see?
O lone one unknown even to the celestials, pervading the world beyond the knowing of those two:
O Red Great Lord, King of Sivapura, Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai —
O our Great Lord, O one who governs me — please call me to you in grace.

Save for those feet which Vishnu sings and praises, I have no other refuge, do you see?
You sought me and claimed me — King of Sivapura, Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai.
To disagree with you and to rejoice with you — to know you is certainty for both of us.
I am withering here. I cannot live, do you see? Grace me by saying "come."

O one who swiftly burned the forts of the fierce demons — I have no other refuge, do you see?
O Dancer who lives at Thillai, King of Sivapura, Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai —
on the day those two tried to behold your boundless extent across the three worlds,
you grew mightily with no beginning and no end.
I cannot live, do you see? Grace me by saying "come."

O you who share your form with her of melody-voiced speech — save for you, I have no other refuge, do you see?
You truly made me your own — King of Sivapura, Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai.
Placing in you this knowing — thought, body, mouth, nose, ear, eye — all of it:
I cannot live on this earth, do you see? Grace me by saying "come."

O you who share your form with her of cotton-soft feet — save for you, I have no other refuge, do you see?
You claimed me purely — King of Sivapura, Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai.
I was afraid, I the dog — I the deceiver who has forgotten through delusion the grace you gave when you claimed me.
I cannot live here, do you see? Grace me by saying "come."

O radiance that lives in the sun — save for those feet, I have no other refuge, do you see?
O King of Sivapura of auspicious, exalted beauty, Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai —
not knowing how to gaze on grace, melt, dissolve within, and live united with you —
I the deluded one cannot live in this world, do you see? Grace me by saying "come."
