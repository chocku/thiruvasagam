பிரார்த்தனைப் பத்து — PIRARTANAI PATTU
==========================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 32
Composed at Thirupperunthurai

Pirartanai Pattu (The Ten of Prayer) follows directly after Kanda Pattu's sealed vision of Thillai. Where every verse of that chapter closed with "I beheld!" — past tense, the seeing accomplished — here every verse is longing, want, and the ache of what is not yet possessed. The poet watches Shiva's devotees: they plunge into the ocean of nectar-grace, their hearts melt endlessly, they bathe and laugh and rejoice. He stands outside like an outcast dog, or like a mind of solid bamboo that cannot melt, or a withered tree, or a one-shouldered hollow gourd. Verse after verse he names what others receive and asks when the same will reach him. The appeal sharpens as the chapter progresses: first lamenting his separation, then naming what he desires — true love that wants nothing, the severing of self in supreme bliss, the tender golden feet — until the final verse envisions what he could be: joining, melting, overflowing, dancing, grace mingled so that bliss itself becomes everything.
