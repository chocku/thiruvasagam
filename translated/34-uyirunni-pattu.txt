உயிருண்ணிப் பத்து — UYIRUNNI PATTU
======================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 34
Composed at Thirupperunthurai

Uyirunni Pattu (The Song of the Soul-Feeder) is a ten-verse hymn of joyous dissolution. "உயிருண்ணி" — the one who devours and absorbs the soul — is Manikkavasagar's name for Shiva in this chapter: he entered the poet's body, mingled with his soul, and will not leave. The opening refrain sets the register: "O Bull-rider, destroyer of karma, who dwells in Thirupperunthurai praised by eloquent bards — O sweet one, which day will I rejoice and feel exalted?" Each verse answers that question. Shiva set a throne for a dog who came to his feet, entered flesh and soul and heart, melted the marrow of the spine, and became the poet himself. The chapter is not anguish but wonder — each verse rings with "I do not know what he has done to me," not as complaint but as awe. By verse 511 the poet renounces everything — fame, wealth, earth, heaven, rebirth — and clings only to the feet of the Lord. The final verses overflow: what shall I call you — honey? Nectar? The poet knows himself only as a remnant, yet asks with quiet certainty: even though I am a sinner, may I not say to you — grant me your grace?
