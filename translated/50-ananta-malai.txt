ஆனந்த மாலை — ANANDA MALAI
====================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 50
Composed at Thillai (Chidambaram)

Ananda Malai (Garland of Bliss) is a seven-verse chapter whose title is richly paradoxical — the content is anything but blissful. Each verse is a cry of desolation: the poet acknowledges that God's grace exists, that others have received it, that even the gods stand praising — and then turns to ask why he, stone-hearted and wretched, has been left behind. The chapter moves through several registers of petition: bewilderment, self-condemnation, reproach, pleading. It closes with the image of jackals turned to thoroughbred horses — a legendary act of grace — and the baffled confession: "I know not what to do." The paradox of the title holds: this garland of bliss is woven from the devotee's longing for it.

1

மின்னே ரனைய    பூங்கழல்கள்    அடைந்தார்    கடந்தார்    வியனுலகம்    பொன்னே ரனைய    மலர்கொண்டு    போற்றா நின்றார்    அமரர் எல்லாம்    கல் நேரனைய    மனக்கடையாய்க்    கழிப்புண்டு    அவலக் கடல்    வீழ்ந்த    என்னேரனையேன்    இனி    உன்னைக் கூடும் வண்ணம்    இயம்பாயே
lightning-like    flower-adorned sacred feet    those who reached / attained    transcended    this vast world    gold-like    with flowers    stood praising    all the immortals / gods    stone-like    of the basest / lowest mind    swept out and wasted    sea of misery    fallen into    one like me — what am I?    now / henceforth    in a manner that unites with you    please tell / speak

**Those who reached your lightning-bright sacred feet have transcended this vast world. All the immortals stand praising you with flowers like gold. But I — stone-hearted, the lowest of the low, cast down and fallen into a sea of misery — what am I like? Tell me now how I may be united with you.**

2

என்னால் அறியாப் பதம்    தந்தாய்    யான தறியாதே    கெட்டேன்    உன்னால்    ஒன்றும் குறைவில்லை    உடையாய்    அடிமைக்கு    ஆர் என்பேன்    பன்னாள்    உன்னைப் பணிந்தேத்தும்    பழைய அடியாரொடும்    கூடாது    தென் நாயகமே    பிற்பட்டிங்கிருந்து    என் நோய்க்கு    விருந்தாயே
a state beyond my knowing    you gave    I, not knowing what I had received    perished / was undone    from you    there is no lack of anything    O Lord / O Possessor of all    for this servant    who am I to say / what shall I claim    for many days    who bow and praise you    the ancient devotees    without joining    O Lord of the South    remaining here, left behind    for my sorrow / grief    became a feast / grew fat upon me

**You gave me a state beyond my knowing — yet I, not knowing what I had received, was undone. From you, O Lord, there is no lack of anything — but who am I to call myself your servant? Left behind here, not joining the ancient devotees who bow and praise you day after day, O Lord of the South — I have become a banquet for my own grief.**

3

சீலம் இன்றி    நோன்பு இன்றி    செறிவே இன்றி    அறிவு இன்றி    தோலின் பாவை    கூத்தாட்டாய்    சுழன்று விழுந்து கிடப்பேனை    மாலும் காட்டி    வழிகாட்டி    வாரா உலக நெறி ஏற    கோலம் காட்டி    ஆண்டானை    கொடியேன்    என்றோ    கூடுவதே
without virtue    without austerity    without steadiness / concentration    without wisdom    body's puppet / puppet of skin    in the dance of this flesh    spinning and fallen, one who lies here    who revealed himself even to Vishnu (மால் — Vishnu the Preserver)    showed the path    to ascend the road of no return (liberation — the world one does not return from)    displaying his divine beauty and form    who claimed me as his own    I, the wicked one    when ever?    shall be united

**Without virtue, without austerity, without steadiness, without wisdom — I am nothing but a puppet of skin, spinning and fallen in this body's dance. Yet he who revealed himself even to Vishnu, who showed the path, who set me upon the road of no return, who displayed his divine form and drew me in as his own — when, wicked one that I am, shall I be united with him?**

4

கெடுவேன்    கெடுமா    கெடுகின்றேன்    கேடு இலாதாய்    பழி கொண்டாய்    படுவேன்    படுவதெல்லாம் நான் பட்டால்    பின்னைப் பயன் என்னே    கொடுமா நரகத்து    அழுந்தாமே    காத்து ஆட்கொள்ளும்    குருமணியே    நடுவாய் நில்லாது ஒழிந்தக்கால்    நன்றோ    எங்கள் நாயகமே
I, the lost one    how greatly lost!    I go on being destroyed    O you who know no destruction    you have taken the blame / dishonor    I will suffer    all there is to suffer — if I endure it all    what use is there afterward?    into the great cruel hell    without sinking    protecting and claiming as your own    O precious Guru-gem    if you stand aside without intervening    is it right / is it good?    O our Lord and Master

**I am lost — utterly lost — I go on being destroyed. O you who cannot be destroyed, you have taken on the blame. If I endure everything there is to endure, what use is it afterward? O precious Guru-gem, protect me and take me as your own so I sink not into the depths of terrible hell. O our Lord — is it right to stand aside and not intervene?**

5

தாயாய் முலையைத் தருவானே    தாராதொழிந்தால்    சவலையாய்    நாயேன்    கழிந்துபோவேனோ    நம்பி    இனித்தான்    நல்குதியே    தாயே என்று    உன் தாளடைந்தேன்    தயா நீ என்பால் இல்லையே    நாயேன் அடிமை    உடனாக    ஆண்டாய்    நான்தான்    வேண்டாவோ
O one who nourishes like a mother giving the breast    if you withhold it and turn away    as a weakling / destitute one    I, the dog (நாயேன்)    shall I simply waste away and perish?    O trusted one / O faithful one    now at last / from now on    please bestow / grant grace    saying "O Mother!"    I have come to your feet    you have no compassion toward me!    I, the dog-servant (நாயேன் அடிமை)    together as / along with    you claimed and ruled    me — am I    not needed / not wanted?

**O you who nourish like a mother giving the breast — if you withhold it and turn away, shall I, the dog, simply waste away and perish? O trusted one, grant your grace at last! Crying "O Mother!" I have come to your feet — yet you have no compassion for me? You brought me in, this dog-servant, as your own — am I not wanted?**

6

கோவே    அருள வேண்டாவோ    கொடியேன்    கெடவே அமையுமே    ஆவா வென்னா விடில்    என்னை    அஞ்சேல் என்பார்    ஆரோதான்    சாவார் எல்லாம்    என்னளவோ    தக்கவாறன்று என்னாரோ    தேவே    தில்லை நடமாடீ    திகைத்தேன்    இனித்தான்    தேற்றாயே
O King!    is it not needed to bestow grace?    I, the wicked one    is it enough that I simply perish?    if you do not cry "Alas!" in concern    me    who will say "Fear not"?    who indeed?    all those who die    are they at my level? / are they all like me?    will no one say "this is not right / not fitting"?    O God!    O dancer at Thillai!    I am confounded / bewildered    now at last    please console / enlighten me

**O King, is it not needed to bestow your grace? Is it enough that I, the wicked one, simply perish? If you do not cry "Alas!" — who will say "Fear not" to me? Are all who die at my level? Will no one say this is not fitting? O God, O dancer at Thillai — I am bewildered. Now at last, console me.**

7

நரியை    குதிரைப் பரியாக்கி    ஞாலமெல்லாம்    நிகழ்வித்துப்    பெரிய தென்னன்    மதுரையெல்லாம்    பிச்ச தேற்றும்    பெருந்துறையாய்    அரிய பொருளே    அவிநாசி    அப்பா    பாண்டி வெள்ளமே    தெரிய அரிய    பரஞ்சோதி    செய்வதொன்றும்    அறியேனே
jackals    transformed into thoroughbred horses    the whole world    causing it to come to pass / making it ring out    the great southern / Pandya king    all of Madurai    spreading the sacred frenzy / divine madness of devotion    O Lord of Perunthurai (Tiruperundurai — where Shiva first appeared to Manikkavasagar)    O rare and precious essence    O Avinashi (the Imperishable — sacred Shiva temple town in Tamil Nadu)    O Father!    O flood / ocean of the Pandya land    difficult to perceive clearly    the Supreme Light (Para Jyoti)    anything to do    I know not

**O Lord of Perunthurai who turned jackals into thoroughbred horses and made it ring out through all the world, who spread the sacred frenzy of devotion through all the great Pandya king's Madurai — O rare and precious essence, O Avinashi the Imperishable, O Father, O flood of Pandya grace, O Supreme Light too deep to fathom — I know not what to do.**
