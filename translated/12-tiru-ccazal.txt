திருச்சாழல் — THIRUCHAZHAL
====================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 12
Composed at Thillai (Chidambaram)

Thiruchazhal ("The Sacred Chazhal") is a dialogue folk-song in Naladith Tharavu Kocchagak Kalippaa metre, composed at Thillai. "Chazhal" is a traditional women's riddle-game: one player poses a puzzling question about Shiva and the other resolves it with a cosmic explanation, closing each answer with the refrain "Chazhal!" One woman challenges the other with Shiva's seeming contradictions — his ash-smearing and serpent-wearing, his coarse loincloth and skull-bowl, his forest-dwelling with no family, his acts of destruction and grace. Her companion answers every riddle by revealing its cosmic necessity: the four Vedas are his loincloth, the halahala poison his gift to the gods, the Natam his protection of the earth from Kali. The arc moves from Shiva's paradoxical appearance and poverty through his great mythological deeds — swallowing the cosmic poison, burning the three cities, teaching under the banyan tree as Dakshinamurthy — to a final revelation: whatever he wears, whatever he eats, his very nature is one who does not know his own greatness. The tone is playful and intimate throughout; the theology is vast.

what he smears · white ash    what he wears · the surging serpent    what he speaks · through the sacred mouth    it seems like the Vedas · don't you see, girl?    the smearing and the speaking    and the wearing · taking all that · me    that Isa · for every soul    has become the very nature · chazhal!

**He smears himself with white ash. He wears the surging serpent. He speaks through the sacred Veda-mouth. Don't you see, girl? Taking all of this — the smearing, the speaking, the wearing — that very Isa has become the inherent nature of every soul. Chazhal!**

**His temple is the burning ground. His fine garment is a tiger skin taken from the kill. He has no mother, no father — he stands utterly alone. Don't you see, girl? Daksha and Eccha    beheading · the assembly of gods    coming all together · them    destroying · what is it, girl?    coming all together · them    graciously destroying · then giving grace    to Eccha · a superior head · furthermore    graciously gave · see! · chazhal!

Brahma and Vishnu    not knowing · in the form of fire    below the very ground · reaching the universe's end    standing there · what is it, girl?    below the very ground · reaching the universe's end    if he had not stood · both of them    through their proud arrogance    would not have given it up · see! · chazhal!

**Without Brahma or Vishnu knowing, he stood as a pillar of fire stretching from below the very ground to the top of the universe. What was that, girl? If he had not stood thus, both of them, in their proud arrogance, would never have given it up. See! Chazhal!**

**Even as he holds the mountain's daughter Parvati on one half of himself, another woman — Ganga — rushes through the force of water into his matted locks. What is that, girl?
