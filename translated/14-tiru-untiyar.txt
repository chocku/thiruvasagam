திருவுந்தியார் — THIRUUNTIYAR
====================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 14
Composed at Thillai (Chidambaram)

Thiruuntiyar ("The Sacred Unthiipara") is a 20-verse work-song in Kalith Thaazhi Sai metre, composed at Thillai. Its theme is "Victory of Knowledge" (ஞான வெற்றி). "உந்தீபற" (Unthiipara) is the work-song refrain — a traditional cry used in women's pounding-songs, meaning roughly "drive it away" or "rise up, be gone." Each verse narrates one act of Shiva's cosmic power, drawn mostly from two great episodes: the burning of the three cities (Tripura) and the destruction of Daksha's sacrifice. In the Tripura story, Shiva bent his cosmic bow and fired a single arrow that burned all three cities at once, the chariot-axle breaking at the critical moment. In the Daksha episode, Veerabhadra and the Bhuta armies wounded every god present — Agni's hands cut, Surya's teeth knocked out, Bhaga's eyes plucked, the Moon's face crushed, Daksha beheaded. Other verses recall Brahma's fifth head cut by Shiva's nail, Ravana's ten heads and twenty arms crushed under Shiva's toe, Indra hiding as a cuckoo, and Shiva standing as the guardian of the sky and beyond. Each verse closes with the double refrain: "Unthiipara! Unthiipara!"

தேரை நிறுத்தி மலையெடுத் தான்சிரம்    ஈரைந்தும் இற்றவா றுந்தீபற    இறுபதும் இற்றதென் றுந்தீபற
theeRai niRuththi malaiyeduth thaanssiram    iiRaindhum iRRavaaR unthiipara    iRupathum iRRathendR unthiipara
stopping him · who lifted the mountain    all ten heads · how they broke · Unthiipara!    and how all twenty arms also broke · Unthiipara!

**Stopping Ravana who lifted the mountain — all his ten heads broke — Unthiipara! And all twenty of his arms broke too — Unthiipara!**

20

ஏகாசமிட்ட இருடிகள் போகாமல்    ஆகாசங்காவலென் றுந்தீபற    அதற்கப்பாலுங் காவலென் றுந்தீபற
eekaasam itta iRudigkaL poogaamal    aagaasangkaavalen unthiipara    athaRkappaalum kaavalen unthiipara
having given space · the rishis · so they would not go    the guardian of the sky · Unthiipara!    and the guardian even beyond that · Unthiipara!

**Having given his sacred space to the rishis so they would not be driven away — the guardian of the sky — Unthiipara! And the guardian even beyond the sky — Unthiipara!**
