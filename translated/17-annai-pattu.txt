அன்னைப் பத்து — ANNAI PATTU
====================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 17
Composed at Perunthurai and Uttara Koshamangai

In this tender folk-song, a young woman is so overwhelmed by Shiva's beauty and paradoxes that she can only cry out "Annē!" — an exclamation of wonder, like calling out to one's mother in amazement. Each of the ten verses describes a different aspect of Shiva: His body red-gold and white-ash-smeared, His speech the Vedas, His drum the primal sound of creation, His heart full of compassion. He is the eternal bridegroom of Perunthurai, the incomprehensible Lord of Uttara Koshamangai, the half-woman ascetic who goes begging for alms. Each description ends with the same helpless cry — "Annē!" — not addressed to anyone in particular, but welling up from a heart overwhelmed by beauty and grace. The final verse turns on a pun: the datura flower (maththam) crowning His head has become the speaker's own madness (maththam).

1

வேத மொழியர்    வெண் நீற்றர்    செம் மேனியர்    நாதப் பறையினர்    அன்னே என்னும்    நாதப் பறையினர்    நான்முகன் மாலுக்கும்    நாதரிந் நாதனார்    அன்னே என்னும்
Veetha mozhiyar    ven niitrar    sem meeniyar    Naathap paraiyinar    Annee ennum    Naathap paraiyinar    naanmugan maalukku-m    Naathar-indh naathanaar    Annee ennum
Veetha = Veda    mozhiyar = whose-speech-is    ven = white    niitrar = ash-wearers    sem = red/golden    meeniyar = bodied-one    naathap-paraiyinar = drum-of-primal-sound-player    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"    naanmugan = Brahma(four-faced)    maalukku-m = and-for-Vishnu    Naathar = Lord    indh-naathanaar = this-Lord    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"

**He whose very speech is the Vedas, smeared in white sacred ash, golden-red of body — He who plays the drum of the primal Nāda — she cries "Annē!" He who plays the drum of the Nāda, the Lord beyond whom even Brahma and Vishnu cannot see — He is the Lord of all lords — she cries "Annē!"**

2

கண்ணஞ் சனத்தார்    கருணைக் கடலினர்    உள்நின் றுருக்குவர்    அன்னே என்னும்    உள்நின் றுருக்கி    உலப்பிலா ஆனந்தக்    கண்ணீர் தருவரால்    அன்னே என்னும்
Kannanj sanathtaar    karunaik katalinnar    Ul-nin rurukkuvar    Annee ennum    Ul-nin rurukkki    ulapilaa aanandha-k    Kanneer tharuvaaraal    Annee ennum
Kannanj-sanathtaar = kohl-lined-eyes-wearer    karunaik-katalinnar = ocean-of-compassion    Ul = inside    nin = standing    urukkuvar = melts-the-heart    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"    ulapilaa = boundless/endless    aanandha = bliss    Kanneer = tears    tharuvaaraal = gives-indeed    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"

**His eyes are lined with kohl; He is an ocean of compassion. He stands within and melts the heart — she cries "Annē!" Standing within, melting the heart, He gives the tears of boundless bliss — she cries "Annē!"**

3

நித்த மணாளர்    நிரம்ப அழகியர்    சித்தத் திருப்பரால்    அன்னே என்னும்    சித்தத் திருப்பவர்    தென்னன் பெரும்துறை    அத்தர் ஆனந்தரால்    அன்னே என்னும்
Niththa manaaḷar    nirampa azhagiyar    Siththath thirupparaal    Annee ennum    Siththath thirupavar    thennan Perunthurai    Aththar aanandharaal    Annee ennum
Niththa = eternal    manaaḷar = bridegroom/Lord    nirampa = fully/completely    azhagiyar = beautiful-one    Siththam = heart/mind    thiruppar-aal = dwells-indeed    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"    Siththath-thirupavar = He-who-dwells-in-the-heart    thennan = of-the-Pandya(South)    Perunthurai = Perunthurai(great-ford)    Aththar = Father/Lord    aanandhar-aal = bliss-indeed    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"

**The eternal bridegroom, completely beautiful — He dwells in the heart — she cries "Annē!" He who dwells in the heart is the Lord of Perunthurai in the Pandya land, the Father who is pure bliss — she cries "Annē!"**

4

ஆடரப் பூணுடை    தோல்பொடிப் பூசிற்றோர்    வேடம் இருந்தவா று    அன்னே என்னும்    வேடம் இருந்தவா    கண்டுகண் டென்னுள்ளம்    வாடும் இதுவென்ன    அன்னே என்னும்
Aatarap puunutai    thool poti puusitru or    Veetam irunthaavaa-ru    Annee ennum    Veetam irunthaavaa    kandu-kandu en-ullam    Vaatum ithuvenna    Annee ennum
Aatara = dancing-serpent    puun-utai = ornament-wearing    thool = hide/skin    poti = powder/ash    puusi = smearing    or = one    Veetam = form/disguise    irunthaavaa-ru = the-manner-of    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"    kandu-kandu = seeing-and-seeing(again-and-again)    en-ullam = my-heart    Vaatum = withers/pines    ithuvenna = what-is-this?    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"

**He wears dancing serpents as His ornament, smeared in ash on a skin — such is the wonder of His form — she cries "Annē!" Seeing that wondrous form again and again, my heart withers away with longing. What is this? — she cries "Annē!"**

5

நீண்ட கரத்தர்    நெறிதரு குஞ்சியர்    பாண்டிநன் னாடரால்    அன்னே என்னும்    பாண்டிநன் னாடர்    பரந்தெழு சிந்தையை    ஆண்டன்பு செய்வரால்    அன்னே என்னும்
Niinda karaththar    neritaru kunjiyar    Paanti-nan naataraal    Annee ennum    Paanti-nan naatar    paranthezhum sinthaiyai    Aant-anbu seivaaraal    Annee ennum
Niinda = long    karaththar = armed/handed    neritaru = path-giving    kunjiyar = matted-locks-wearer    Paanti-nan-naatar = Lord-of-the-fair-Pandya-land    aal = indeed    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"    paranthezhum = rising-and-spreading    sinthai = thought/mind    Aant = ruling    anbu = love    seivaaraal = does-indeed    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"

**He of long arms, whose matted locks bestow the path of liberation — the Lord of the fair Pandya land — she cries "Annē!" The Lord of the fair Pandya land rules over my spreading, rising thoughts and fills them with love — she cries "Annē!"**

6

உன்னற் கரியசீர்    உத்தர மங்கையர்    மன்னுவ தென்நெஞ்சில்    அன்னே என்னும்    மன்னுவ தென்நெஞ்சில்    மாலயன் காண்கிலார்    என்ன அதியசம்    அன்னே என்னும்
Unnar kariya-siir    Utthara Mangaiyar    Mannuva then-nenjil    Annee ennum    Mannuva then-nenjil    Maalayan kaangilaar    Enna athiyasam    Annee ennum
Unnar-kariya = hard-to-contemplate    siir = glory/grace    Utthara-Mangaiyar = Lord-of-Uttara-Koshamangai    Mannuva = abides    then-nenjil = in-my-heart    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"    Maal = Vishnu    ayan = Brahma    kaangilaar = could-not-see/behold    Enna = what-a    athiyasam = wonder/marvel    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"

**He of incomprehensible glory, the Lord of Uttara Koshamangai — He abides in my heart — she cries "Annē!" He who abides in my heart, whom neither Vishnu nor Brahma could ever behold — what a wonder this is! — she cries "Annē!"**

7

வெள்ளைக் கலிங்கத்தர்    வெண்திரு முண்டத்தர்    பள்ளிக்குப் பாயத்தர்    அன்னே என்னும்    பள்ளிக்குப் பாயத்தர்    பாய்பரி மேற்கொண்டு    என் உள்ளங் கவர்வரால்    அன்னே என்னும்
Vellai-k kalingaththar    ven-thiru mundaththar    Pallikkup paayaththar    Annee ennum    Pallikkup paayaththar    paay-pari meerkontu    En ullam kavar-vaaraal    Annee ennum
Vellai = white    kalingam = garment    ven = white    thiru = sacred    mundam = skull    Pallikku = for-resting    paayam = mat    thar = having    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"    paay-pari = leaping-horse    meerkontu = mounting/riding    En = my    ullam = heart    kavar-vaaraal = steals-indeed    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"

**He wears white garments and bears a sacred white skull. He rests on a simple mat — she cries "Annē!" He who rests on a mat, yet mounts a leaping horse — He steals my heart away — she cries "Annē!"**

8

தாளி அறுகினர்    சந்தனச் சாந்தினர்    ஆளெம்மை ஆள்வரால்    அன்னே என்னும்    ஆளெம்மை ஆளும்    அடிகளார் தங்கையில்    தாள மிருந்தவா று    அன்னே என்னும்
Thaazhi aruginar    santhanach chaanthinnar    Aalemmai aaḷvaaraal    Annee ennum    Aalemmai aaḷum    Atigaḷaar thangaiyil    Thaalam irunthaavaa-ru    Annee ennum
Thaazhi = sedge-grass    arugam = arugam-grass    saanthinnar = sandalwood-paste-anointed    Aal = Lord    emmai = us    aaḷvaaraal = rules-indeed    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"    Atigaḷaar = Lord/the-sacred-footed-one    thangaiyil = in-His-hand    Thaalam = rhythm-cymbal/tala    irunthaavaa-ru = manner-of-holding    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"

**He is adorned with sedge and arugam grass and anointed with sandalwood paste. He is our Lord who rules over us — she cries "Annē!" He who rules over us — see how our Lord holds the rhythm in His hand as He dances! — she cries "Annē!"**

9

தையலோர் பங்கினர்    தாபத வேடத்தர்    ஐயம் புகுவரால்    அன்னே என்னும்    ஐயம் புகுந்தவர்    போதலும் என்னுள்ளம்    நையுமிது வென்னே    அன்னே என்னும்
Thaiyyal-or panginar    thaapatha veetathar    Aiyam puguvaaraal    Annee ennum    Aiyam puguntavar    poothalum en-ullam    Naiyum ithu-venne    Annee ennum
Thaiyyal = woman    or = one    panginar = half-body-sharer(Ardhanarishvara)    thaapatha = ascetic    veetathar = form-wearer    Aiyam = alms    puguvaaraal = goes-begging-indeed    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"    puguntavar = having-entered(to-beg)    poothalum = when-He-departs    en-ullam = my-heart    Naiyum = melts/pines    ithu-venne = what-is-this?    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"

**He shares His body with a beautiful woman as His other half, yet wears the guise of an ascetic. He goes begging for alms — she cries "Annē!" When He who came begging departs, my heart melts away. What is this? — she cries "Annē!"**

கொன்றை மதியமும்    கூவிள மத்தமும்    துன்றிய சென்னியர்    அன்னே என்னும்    துன்றிய சென்னியின்    மத்தம்உன் மத்தமே    இன்றெனக் கானவா று    அன்னே என்னும்
Konrai mathiyam-um    kuuviḷa maththam-um    Thunriya senniyar    Annee ennum    Thunriya senniyil    maththam-un maththamee    Intrenak kaanavaaru    Annee ennum
Konrai = golden-shower-flower    mathiyam = crescent-moon    kuuviḷam = bilva-leaf    maththam = datura-flower(also:madness)    Thunriya = dense/crowded    senniyar = crowned-head    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"    maththam = datura(pun:madness)    un = your    maththamee = very-madness/intoxication-itself    Intrenak = for-me-today    kaanavaaru = how-it-has-become    Annee-ennum = she-cries-"Annē!"

**His crowded crown bears konrai flowers, the crescent moon, bilva leaves, and datura blooms — she cries "Annē!" The datura (maththam) on His crowned head — that very datura has become my own madness (maththam) today. See how it has happened! — she cries "Annē!"**
