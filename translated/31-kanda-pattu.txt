கண்ட பத்து — KANDA PATTU
============================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 31
Composed at Thillai

Kanda Pattu (The Ten of Beholding) carries the subtitle "Vision of the Dance" (Niruttha Daricanam). It marks a turning point: every previous chapter asked, longed, or cried out; every verse here closes with "I beheld!" — past tense, a completed vision. The setting shifts from Thirupperunthurai to Thillai (Chidambaram), the great hall of Shiva's cosmic dance. What is beheld is not merely Shiva's form but the entire sequence of actions by which he claimed the poet — entering the womb before form existed, clearing the mind, severing karma, binding him with the rope of the mind to his feet, making others call him a madman. Each verse names what Shiva did and closes: I saw it happen. It was at Thillai.
