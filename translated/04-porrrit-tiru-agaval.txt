போற்றித் திருவகவல் — POTRITH THIRUAGAVAL
==========================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 4
Composed at Thillai (Chidambaram)

Pottri-t-Tiru-Agaval — 'The Sacred Homage Hymn' — is the fourth chapter of Thiruvasagam, composed in agaval metre at Thillai (Chidambaram). The poem is structured in twelve long sections, each a sustained movement of praise. It opens with the ancient episode of Vishnu searching for Shiva's feet and crown, a myth establishing Shiva's incomprehensible supremacy. The poem then catalogues the soul's journey through all stages of embodied existence — from womb to birth to the obstacles and attachments of worldly life — before arriving at the moment of grace and total surrender. The heart of the hymn is a long cascade of 'Pottri' (Homage) refrains, each naming a different attribute, sacred site, or gracious act of Shiva, accumulating into an overwhelming act of devotion. It closes with the triumphant double refrain: 'Homage! Homage! — Victory! Victory! — Homage!'

**As Brahma foremost and all celestials rose in worship,
as the sages of four directions bloomed open with every sense —
Vishnu, the sacred tall one with his radiant crown,
who praised you and burned with longing to know your foot and crown,

And yet — in this wide ocean-world, those same feet are made easy to praise.**

**Yet without slipping — not wavering from what they had seized —
like wax that has seen fire,
I worshipped. Heart melted. Wept. Body trembled.
Danced and cried aloud, sang and praised —
stubborn and foolish, refusing to let go of what I had found.
I became exactly that: love that never breaks, that has no gap.
Like a nail driven into green wood,
the soft oozing spread wider and wider, swirling like the sea —
heart softening, becoming yielding, the true body shivering —
while the world laughed and called me a madman.
I abandoned shame. I wore the village's blame as an ornament.
Without bending, I ran — having lost all cleverness, seized by divine bewilderment —
that very state becoming the supreme wonder, the highest path.
Like a calf's mind, crying and panicking,
I never thought of any other deity even in a dream.
Then the One of the Formless Beyond came to earth
and graced me as the Supreme Guru.
Not scorning that greatness as small,
I followed the pair of sacred feet like a shadow that knows no separation —
before and behind, without resentment, in that direction —
bones dissolving, melting, softening again and again, longing —
until the river called love overflowed its banks,
all good senses united, crying out: "O Natha! O Lord!" —
words stumbling, body hair standing on end —
hands folded like flower-buds, heart blooming open —
eyes filled with joy, fine tears welling up.

For those who day by day cultivate that unfading love —
you became their mother and nurtured them. Homage!
You who become the true Brahmin — the knower of truth — so that karma is destroyed,
who can give it by your own hand — O God. Homage!**
