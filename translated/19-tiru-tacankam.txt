திருத்தசாங்கம் — THIRUTHASAANGAM
====================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 19
Composed at Perunthurai and Uttara Koshamangai

Thiruthasaangam (The Ten Sacred Royal Attributes) is the final folk-form song of the Thiruvasagam and the culmination of Group C. It follows an ancient Tamil panegyric tradition in which a king's glory is proclaimed through ten royal attributes: Name, Country, Town, River, Mountain, Horse, Army, Drum, Garland, and Flag. Manikkavasagar applies this tradition in full to Shiva, devoting one stanza to each attribute. In each stanza the poet addresses a bird — young parrots, a dove, a green parrot — and asks it to proclaim one of Shiva's royal attributes. The answers unfold: His name is "Our Lord, Lord of all gods"; His country is the southern Pandya land; His town is Uttara Koshamangai; His river is the Ganga that descended from heaven to wash away the impurities of the heart; His mountain grants liberation with a sword of light; He rides a heavenly horse attended by divine women; His army is the lance of grace that drives out the three impurities (ego, karma, illusion); His drum is the mighty drum of the primal Nāda; His garland is of thaazhi and arugam grass; and His flag bears the image of the flawless bull Nandi.

Eeraar = beautiful/fair    iḷang-kuyilee = young-parrot    engaḷ = our    Perunthurai-koon = Lord-of-Perunthurai

**[Name — பெயர்] O beautiful young parrot! Choose carefully and proclaim the glorious sacred name of our Lord of Perunthurai. Just as Ārūran (Sundarar) proclaimed it, just as even Brahma of the white lotus and Vishnu of the milk ocean would say — the name is: "Our Lord, Lord of all gods."**

**[Country — நாடு] O faultless sweet-voiced emerald parrot! Proclaim the country of Him who is the sovereign Lord of all seven worlds, who has servants everywhere. His country is the land of Him who rules with love and bestows unceasing grace on His devoted ones — know it clearly, it is forever the southern Pandya land.**

**[Town — ஊர்] O dove in the pollen-filled flower grove! Tell us the dwelling place of Him who rules us, He who has a dancing woman as His half (Ardhanarishvara). Having removed all faults, all His devotees on earth celebrate it as a Sivapuram — a celestial city of Shiva. That town is Uttara Koshamangai.**

It is the river that descended from heaven, flowing down to wash away all impurity from the mind — the river of Him who bestows the vision of bliss. That is His river.**

**[Mountain — மலை] O parrot with a flame-of-the-forest red beak! Proclaim the mountain where the faultless Lord of Perunthurai abides. Examine and understand clearly — it is the mountain that wields a sword of light to drive away the darkness in the heart, granting the rare liberation of supreme bliss. That is the mountain of grace.**

**[Army — படை] O parrot of honey-cane sweet speech! Proclaim the army of the faultless Lord of Perunthurai that conquers all enemies. It melts the hearts of the worthy without ever touching filth, and leaps upon the three impurities — ego, karma, and illusion — to drive them out. See clearly — that lance is the weapon to take up.**
