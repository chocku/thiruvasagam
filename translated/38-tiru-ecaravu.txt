திருவேசறவு — TIRU ECARAVU
====================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 38
Composed at Thirupperunthurai

Tiru Ecaravu (The Song of Marveling) is Manikkavasagar's litany of gratitude and astonishment. Each verse recounts a specific act of divine grace — drawing out a heart hard as iron, showing the sweetness of sugarcane, turning jackals into great horses, saying "Fear not!," entering the heart uninvited — and closes with the same rhetorical question: "Is this not so?" (அன்றே). The tone is not triumphant but tenderly amazed: how did this happen? How did one as lowly as I receive all this? The chapter moves through ten acts of grace, from the first miracle of being drawn in (verse 1) to the final wonder of God entering the heart of his own accord and bestowing the sacred mantra (verse 10). It is one of the most intimate chapters in the collection — less a praise hymn than a whispered conversation with grace itself.
