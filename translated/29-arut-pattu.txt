அருட்பத்து — ARUT PATTU
==========================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 29
Composed at Thirupperunthurai

Arut Pattu (The Ten of Grace) carries the subtitle "Purification of the Great Illusion" (Mahaamaayaa Sutti). Every verse is anchored to a single image: the kuruntham tree in full bloom at Thirupperunthurai, beneath which Shiva dwells in his glory. The request at the end of each verse is devastatingly modest — not liberation, not union, not even a vision: just one word from Shiva, "what is it?" — his acknowledging the servant who calls to him. Nine verses hold this longing steady. The tenth breaks the pattern: the servant's call is answered not with "what is it?" but with "look — this is the path into Kailash. Come here."

**Glorious One of Thirupperunthurai surrounded by perfect groves, where the kuruntham blossoms in full flower —
thinking of how you dwell there, meditating without distraction, saying again and again "my Lord, our Lord" —
when this servant calls with rare devotion and longing, standing in the ocean of waves:
"Look — this is the fitting path into Kailash. Come here" — please grace me by saying this.**
