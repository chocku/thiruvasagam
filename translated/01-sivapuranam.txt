சிவபுராணம் — SIVAPURANAM
==========================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 1
Composed at Thiruperundurai

Sivapuranam — 'The Sacred Story of Shiva' — is the opening hymn of Thiruvasagam, composed in agaval metre at Thiruperundurai. The poem opens with an invocatory series of short vaazhka ('long live'), velka ('may they triumph'), and pottri ('homage') acclamations for the sacred name Namashivaya and Shiva's feet and anklets (v1–16), then names the poet's intention: to recite this poem with a rejoicing heart so all past karma may be utterly exhausted (v17–20). The theological heart is a vast confession — the soul has passed through every form of existence, from grass and worm to stone, human, ghost, sage, and god, and is now utterly exhausted (v26–31). The second half is a sustained direct address to Shiva, piling up paradoxes — fierce and gentle, light and dense darkness, all things and none — and culminating in the tender image of grace welling up like honey in the hearts of devoted ones (v46–48). The poem closes with a promise: those who understand and recite this song will have their falsehood perish, will not return to the cycle of karmic birth, and will reach Sivapuri to dwell at Shiva's feet, praised by all (v86–95).
