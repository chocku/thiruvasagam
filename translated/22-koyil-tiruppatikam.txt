கோயில் திருப்பதிகம் — KOYIL TIRUPPATIKAM
==========================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 22
Composed at Thirupperunthurai

Koyil Tiruppatikam (The Shrine Tiruppatikam) is a deeply personal hymn to Shiva at Thirupperunthurai. Each of the ten verses closes with the refrain "Thiruperunthurai urai Sivane" (O Shiva who dwells at holy Thirupperunthurai). Where the cuckoo-song and dawn-hymn before it were full of tender longing, this chapter is quietly intense — Manikkavasagar addresses Shiva as the Supreme Light welling up within him, the Nectar that shut the door of the senses, the Fire hidden within water, the Hidden Form inside the five elements. He twice confesses that he has "no recompense" for what Shiva has given him. The chapter reaches its climax in verse 397 with a stunning paradox: "You gave your very Self — you took my very self — O Sankara, who are the clever ones who understand this exchange?"

யாது நீ போவதோர் வகை எனக்கருளாய்    வந்து நின் இணையடி தந்தே

Jothiyaay thondrum uruvame    aruvaam oruvane solludharku ariya
Aadiye naduuve andame    bandam arukkum aananda maa-kadale
Theethila nanmait thiruvaruL kundre    Thiruperunthurai urai Sivane
Yaadhu nee povadhor vagai enakku aruLaai    vandhu nin iNaiyadi thande

jothiyaay = as Light | thondrum = that appears | uruvame = O Form | aruvaam = who is formless | oruvane = O One | solludharku = to put in words | ariya = hard | aadiye = O Beginning | naduuve = O Middle | andame = O End | bandam = bondage | arukkum = that severs | aananda = bliss | maa-kadal = great sea | theethila = without evil | nanmai = goodness | thiruvaruL = divine grace | kundre = O Mountain | Thiruperunthurai-urai = dwells at Thirupperunthurai | Sivane = O Shiva | yaadhu = what | nee = you | povadhu = going | oor-vagai = a way | enakku-aruLaai = please grant me | vandhu = come | nin = your | iNaiyadi = paired feet | thande = having given

**O Form that appears as Light, O One who is also the Formless — beyond words to say — O Beginning, Middle, and End, O great sea of bliss that severs all bondage — O Mountain of faultless goodness and divine grace, O Shiva of holy Thirupperunthurai — grant me the way by which you depart; come and give me your divine feet!**

தந்தது உன் தன்னை    கொண்டது என் தன்னை சங்கரா ஆர்கொலோ சதுரர்
அந்தம் ஒன்றில்லா ஆனந்தம் பெற்றேன்    யாது நீ பெற்றது ஒன்று என்பால்
சிந்தையே கோயில் கொண்ட எம்பெருமான்    திருப்பெருந்துறை யுறை சிவனே
எந்தையே ஈசா    உடல் இடங் கொண்டாய் யான் இதற்கு இலன் ஓர்கைம்மாறே

Thandhadu un-thannai    kondadu en-thannai Sangaraa aarkolo sadurer
Andham ondru-illa aanandham petreen    yaadhu nee-pettra dhondru enpaal
Sindhayee koyil koNda emberumaaan    Thiruperunthurai urai Sivane
Enthaiye Eesha    udal idang kondaai yaan idharku ilan oor kaimmaare

thandhadu = what was given | un-thannai = your very self | kondadu = what was taken | en-thannai = my very self | Sangaraa = O Sankara | aarkolo = who indeed | sadurer = are the clever ones | andham = end | ondru-illa = without one | aanandham = bliss | petreen = I gained | yaadhu = what | nee = you | pettra = gained | dhondru = a thing | enpaal = from me | sindhayee = the mind itself | koyil-koNda = made as your temple | emberumaaan = O our Lord | Thiruperunthurai-urai = dwells at Thirupperunthurai | Sivane = O Shiva | enthaiye = O my Father | Eesha = O Lord | udal = body | idang = place | kondaai = you took | yaan = I | idharku = for this | ilan = do not have | oor = one | kaimmaare = recompense

**You gave your very Self — you took my very self — O Sankara, who are the clever ones who understand this exchange? I gained endless, boundless bliss — but what did you gain from me in return? O our Lord who made my very mind your temple, O Shiva of holy Thirupperunthurai — O my Father, O Lord, you have taken my body as your abode; I have not a single thing to offer you in return!**
