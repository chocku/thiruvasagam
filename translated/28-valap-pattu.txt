வாழாப் பத்து — VALAAP PATTU
================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 28
Composed at Thirupperunthurai

Valaap Pattu (The Ten of "I Cannot Live") carries the subtitle "Means to Liberation" (Mutti Upaayam). The speaker is stripped of all alternatives — "save for you I have no other refuge, do you see?" — and makes a single demand: grace me by saying "come." Each verse invokes Shiva as King of Sivapura and as Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai, then presses a specific wound: the poet withers; he has forgotten the grace he was given; he does not know how to melt and live united; he refuses to worship anyone else. Some verses introduce Parvati — she of melody-voiced speech, cotton-soft feet, ball-playing fingers, faultless words — as the one who shares Shiva's form, making even her a reason why there is no other refuge. The chapter is among the most compressed acts of surrender in the Thiruvasagam: every verse is a direct address, every line a refusal to look elsewhere.

**O our Supreme One who spreads as earth and sky — I have no other refuge, do you see?
O one resplendent with glory, King of Sivapura, Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai —

Grace me by saying "come."**

**O you who share your form with her of faultless, praiseworthy words — save for you, I have no other refuge, do you see?
O one adorned with the bountiful moon, King of Sivapura, Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai —
shall I worship others? Shall I praise others? Shall I think of any as my support? Tell me!
O one whose mount is a young bull — I cannot live, do you see? Grace me by saying "come."**
