திருப்பொன்னூசல் — THIRUPPONNUSAL
====================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 16
Composed at Uttara Koshamangai

Thirupponnusal ("The Golden Swing-Song") is a 9-verse swing-song in which women sing Shiva's praises while swinging on a golden swing, each verse closing with "shall we swing on the golden swing?" The chapter is centered on the sacred site of Uttara Koshamangai (one of Shiva's great shrines in the deep south), while also praising Idaimarudu. The swing itself — coral posts, pearl ropes, golden plank — becomes an image of the cosmic beauty in which Shiva's grace is offered. He is Neelakanta who ate the cosmic poison, Ardhanarishvara with Parvati as half, the three-eyed Lord whose fresh-flower feet even Narayana cannot find, the one who dwells in the hearts of devotees as flowing ambrosia. Each verse is addressed to a companion — "O women with battle-spear eyes," "O you with a peacock's gait," "O you with bright-white bangles" — gathering them all into the shared joy of worship through play.

**The one with three shining eyes — whose flower-feet even the immortal sky-dwelling gods cannot see. O you who walk like a fine-bred peacock — shall we swing on the golden swing?**

**"Is it male? Is it neuter? Is it female?" — the god that both Brahma and Vishnu could not discern. By compassion he graciously claimed the entire assembly of gods without shame, to save them. O you with adorned firm breasts — singing his qualities — shall we swing on the golden swing?**
