பிடித்த பத்து — PIDITTA PATTU
=================================
By Manikkavasagar | Thiruvasagam, Chapter 37
Composed at Thirupperunthurai

Piditta Pattu (The Song of Holding Fast) is Manikkavasagar's defiant declaration that having once grasped Shiva, he will not let go. Every verse ends with the same question turned into a taunt: "I have grasped you firmly — where can you go now?" Each verse names a different circumstance of this holding: from exhaustion, from darkness, at death itself, following Shiva through all his wandering, right here and now. The epithets that open each verse are extraordinarily varied — King of the celestials, pure awareness without end, peerless jewel, flame impossible to describe, fruit that fell of itself, Mother, Father, Trickster, Madman, Bliss itself. Yet every verse converges on the same act of grasping. What makes this chapter remarkable is its confidence: this is not the poet pleading to be held but declaring that he is the one doing the holding. The self-abasement is still present — worm-headed outcast, filthy one, powerless one — but it serves a different purpose: it makes the grasping all the more astonishing. The most wretched person imaginable has closed his fist around God and demands to know where God thinks he is going.

புன்புலால் யாக்கை புரைபுரை கனிய பொன்னெடும் கோயிலாப் புகுந்து    என்என்பெலாம் உருக்கி எளியை ஆய் ஆண்ட ஈசனே மாசிலா மணியே    துன்பமே பிறப்பே இறப்பொடு மயக்காம் தொடக்கெலாம் அறுத்த நல்சோதீ    இன்பமே உன்னைச் சிக்கெனப் பிடித்தேன் எங்கு எழுந்தருளுவது இனியே

**You entered this mean flesh-stinking body, ripening it layer by layer into a tall golden temple, melted all my bones, and became the accessible Lord who ruled over me. O flawless jewel! O good light that cut all the bonds of suffering, birth, death, and delusion! O Bliss! I have grasped you firmly — where can you go now?**
