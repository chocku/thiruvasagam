Thirupalliyezhuchchi

திருப்பள்ளியெழுச்சி
Chapter 20 · Composed at Thirupperunthurai (Āvudaiyār Kōvil)
LongingWonder
← Ch. 19 All Chapters Ch. 21 →

This is the sacred dawn-hymn of the Thiruvasagam — the song sung at the break of day to rouse Shiva from sleep at the holy shrine of Thirupperunthurai. As Manikkavasagar stands at the temple in the pre-dawn darkness, the world is waking around him: cuckoos call, roosters crow, conches sound, stars fade, and the sun rises. Around him worshippers have gathered on every side — veena players, Veda chanters, those carrying flower garlands, those weeping, those trembling with devotion, those raising folded hands to their foreheads in prayer. Each of the ten stanzas ends with the same yearning refrain: "O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!"

1

போற்றி! என்வாழ் முதல் ஆகிய பொருளே! · புலர்ந்தது · பூங்கழற் கிணைதுணை மலர்கொண்டு · ஏற்றி நின் திருமுகத்து எமக்கருள் மலரும் · எழில்நகை கொண்டு நின் திருவடி தொழுகோம்

Pootri! en-vaazh-mudal-aagiya-poruḷee · puḷarnthathu · puung-kazhar-kinai-thunai-malar-kontu · Eetri-nin-thirumugaththu-emakkuruḷ-malarum · ezhil-nagai-kontu-nin-thiruvati-thozhugoom

Pootri = praise-be · en-vaazh = my-life · mudal = source · poruḷee = O-essence · puḷarnthathu = dawn-has-broken · malar = flowers · thiruvati = sacred-feet · thozhugoom = let-us-worship

Homage! O Essence, the very source and foundation of my life — dawn has broken! Taking matching pairs of flowers for your sacred lotus feet, lifting them up before your sacred face, receiving the beautiful grace-blooming smile you pour on us — let us worship your sacred feet! O great Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai! O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!
2

அருணன் இந்திரன் திசை அணுகினன் · இருள்போய் அகன்றது · உதயம் நின் மலர்த் திருமுகத்தின் · கருணையின் சூரியன் எழ எழ · நயனக் கடிமலர் மலர

Arunan-Intiran-thisai-anuginan · iruḷ-poooy-aganrthathu · utayam-nin-malar-thirumugaththin · Karunayin-suuriyan-ezha-ezha · nayanam-kati-malar-malara

Arunan = dawn-charioteer · iruḷ = darkness · utayam = sunrise · Karunayin = of-compassion · suuriyan = sun · malar = to-bloom

Aruna, the red charioteer of dawn, has approached the eastern direction! Darkness has gone and dispersed. As the sun of Your compassion rises from Your blooming sacred face, the fragrant eye-like flowers bloom fully open — and our beautiful-eyed Lord appears! O great Shiva dwelling at Thirupperunthurai — O mountain of bliss! Please arise from your sacred rest!
3

கூவின பூங்குயில் · கூவின கோழி · குருகுகள் இயம்பின · இயம்பின சங்கம் · ஓவின தாரகை ஒளி · ஒளி உதயத்து ஒருப்படுகின்றது

Kuuvina-puung-kuyil · kuuvina-koozhi · kurukugaḷ-iyampina · iyampina-shangam · Oovina-thaarakai-oḷi · oḷi-utayaththu-oruppatugintrathu

Kuuvina = have-called · puung-kuyil = flower-cuckoos · koozhi = roosters · shangam = conches · thaarakai = stars · oḷi = light · utayam = sunrise

The flower-cuckoos have called! The roosters have called! The egrets have cried out! The conches have sounded! The starlight has faded; the light of sunrise is gathering together. O God, with love, show us your pair of sacred feet! O great Shiva — hard for all the world to know, yet easy and near for us! O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!
4

இன்னிசை வீணையர் யாழினர் ஒருபால் · இருக்கொடு தோத்திரம் இயம்பினர் ஒருபால் · துன்னிய பிணைமலர்க் கையினர் ஒருபால் · தொழுகையர் அழுகையர் துவள்கையர் ஒருபால்

Innisai-viinayar-yaazhinnar-orupaal · irukkoṭu-thoththiram-iyampinam-orupaal · Thunniya-pinai-malark-kaiyinar-orupaal · thozhugaiyar-azhugaiyar-thuvaḷgaiyar-orupaal

Innisai = sweet-music · viinayar = veena-players · orupaal = one-side · thozhugaiyar = worshipping-ones · azhugaiyar = weeping-ones · thuvaḷgaiyar = trembling-ones

On one side, sweet-music players with veenas and harps! On one side, those reciting the Rig Veda and praise hymns! On one side, those holding tightly bound flower-garlands! On one side, worshipping ones, weeping ones, trembling ones! On one side, those raising their joined hands in prayer! O great Shiva — claim even me as your servant and bestow your sweet grace! O our Lord, please arise from your sacred rest!
